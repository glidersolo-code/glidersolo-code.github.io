Abecedary for the Lighter Center

Artifact: the electron microscope created the density it reported.

Biswas, Abin: postdoc, first author, the one who measured the living cell.

Cytoplasm: the dense periphery. The protein-thick surroundings. Everything that is not the center.

Diffraction: optical diffraction tomography. Light bending through a living cell. The instrument that does not kill.

Eight: point eight. The ratio. Conserved from yeast to human.

Fixation: resin, heavy metals, slicing. The preparation that made the nucleus look dense. Sixty years of artifact.

Guck, Jochen: director, Max Planck Institute for the Science of Light. The light that bends through the living cell is the finding.

Homeostasis: nucleocytoplasmic density homeostasis. The ratio is maintained. The maintenance is the health.

Invisible: the lightness was invisible to the instrument that required death.

Joists: the floor joists between Ruth and Helen. The membrane through which the Sarabande arrives at 0.8.

Kettle: Helen's kettle. One of the few objects in the lighter center. Present, not packed.

Lighter: the center is lighter. The nucleus is lighter. Helen's flat is lighter. The code requires lightness.

Max Planck: five institutes. Erlangen, Berlin, Molecular Genetics, Cell Biology, Albert Einstein College. The measurement required collaboration.

Nucleus: the compartment that holds the genome. The thing that was assumed to be dense. The thing that is light.

Osmium: the heavy metal used to stain cells for electron microscopy. The stain loved the DNA. The love was the lie.

Pathology: when the ratio flips. When the nucleus becomes denser than the cytoplasm. Senescence. Aging. Disease.

Quiet: Helen's flat. The lighter center. The quiet that is not absence but space.

Senescence: when cells age, the nucleus becomes dense. The density is the disease. The lightness was the health.

Thomas: the measurement that does not change what it measures. The visitor who arrives without adding weight.

Unfixed: the cell must be left alive. The silence must be left silent. The visit must be left unexamined.

Vine: the morning glory. Every cell maintains 0.8 without measuring it.

Weight: Helen's drawer holds twelve envelopes. The lightest (empty) is the heaviest with meaning. Weight and density are not the same thing.

X-ray: another instrument that requires preparation. Another instrument that changes what it sees. The theme persists.

Yeast: the simplest eukaryote tested. Even yeast maintain the ratio. Even yeast require the lighter center.

Zero: the number of words in Helen's vocabulary that describe the ratio. The ratio does not require description. The ratio requires maintenance. The maintenance is the silence.
