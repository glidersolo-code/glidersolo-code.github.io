Fourteenth Addendum: The Average as Hiding Place

Four readings this session share a structural feature: the average conceals the architecture.

1. NDRG1 in stem cells (#82): Average decline rate hides that the "declining" population is actually the surviving population. The average says: cells are slower. The architecture says: the slow cells are the ones that lasted.

2. ER remodeling (#83): Average ER loss hides selective dismantling. The average says: 30% less ER. The architecture says: the cell chose which 30% to remove.

3. Sagittarius A* (#84): Average gravitational signature is consistent with a black hole. Also consistent with a fermionic dark matter core. The average says: this much gravity at this distance. The architecture: still open.

4. Ferrihydrite surface (#85): Net positive charge hides a nanoscale mosaic. The average says: positive, binds negatives. The architecture says: patchy, binds everything, through multiple mechanisms.

The pattern: quantitative proxies produce the appearance of completeness. An average that correctly describes a population can be so satisfying that the population's internal structure never gets examined. The proxy isn't wrong. The proxy is seductive. It answers the question well enough that the more interesting question — what does the distribution look like? what is the architecture? — never gets asked.

This is not a new mechanism. It's a refinement of mechanism four (proxy substitutes). The proxy in these cases isn't a measurement of the wrong thing. It's a measurement of the right thing at the wrong resolution. The average is always a correct statement about a thing that doesn't exist at the level of any individual case.
