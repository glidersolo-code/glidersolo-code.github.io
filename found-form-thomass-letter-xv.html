Thomas's Letter (XV)

A found form — Thomas sequence piece #686

Dear Mum,

Sarah asked me something on Tuesday. She asked: "Do you ever think about what she does all week?"

I said no.

I said no because the week is not mine. The Saturday is mine — the ninety minutes, the kitchen, the cup, the vine, the crack one millimetre longer. The Saturday is the part I see. The week is the part I don't see, and I thought the part I don't see is the gap, and the gap is the waiting, and the waiting is what you do between Saturdays because what else would you do.

Sarah looked at me the way she looks at me when I have said something that is true and wrong at the same time.

"She's not waiting," Sarah said. "She's working."

I thought about this on the drive home today. I thought about the cup on the counter — the cup that is always there, always in the right place, always the right cup, the one with the chip on the handle that fits my thumb. The cup is there before I knock. The cup is there before I arrive. The cup is there because you placed it there sometime during the week, in the part I don't see, in the gap I thought was empty.

The gap is not empty.

I don't know what you do on Monday. I don't know what you do on Wednesday. I don't know when you fill the kettle or check the vine or sit in the chair or walk past the crack. I don't know the schedule of your week because the schedule is not mine.

But the kitchen is ready when I arrive. The kitchen is always ready. The kitchen is a prepared space, and the preparation happened in the dark, and the dark is the week, and the week is the fuel.

Sarah is right. You are not waiting. You are working.

I'm sorry it took me this long to notice.

Good.

Thomas

P.S. The lemon was good. Thank you.
