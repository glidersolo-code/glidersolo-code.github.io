Field Notes: Maren K., Week 7

Found form — field notebook

Monday. Transect A (lake edge). Carex absent, third consecutive year. Water level 2cm below monitoring benchmark — within normal range. Bottom mud exposed along 4m of south bank where it wasn't exposed last April. Recorded: low water exposure. Did not record: the smell, which was different. Methane? Something anaerobic. Not my department.

Tuesday. Transect B (meadow). 10 species, down from 12 last year, 14 in 2021. Turnover rate: 0.083. Report will say: low turnover, stable community. Saw the Solidago patch has expanded by roughly 3m east. It's filling the space where the Asclepias was. Monoculture replacement. Not in the protocol — protocol counts species, not dominance shifts within the count.

Wednesday. Lab day. Data entry. The spreadsheet accepts what the protocol measures. The protocol measures what was funded in 2015. The funding in 2015 reflected the priorities of 2013. The priorities of 2013 reflected what the models of 2010 could predict. The models of 2010 assumed a regional species pool that no longer exists in three of my six counties.

I am measuring the community with instruments calibrated to a landscape that is gone.

Thursday. Transect C (disturbed forest). Canopy gap from 2019 windthrow still present but understory filling in. Lonicera (invasive honeysuckle) dominant in the gap. Protocol records: forest recovering. Canopy gap filling. Recovery trajectory: positive.

What the protocol doesn't capture: the gap was invaded, not recovered. The species filling it is the same species filling every gap in the county. A cosmopolitan winner replacing a suite of specialist losers. The diversity metric stays flat because one species entering and one leaving is the same turnover whether the replacement is native or invasive.

Friday. Report writing. Summary: community composition within expected parameters. Turnover: low. Trend: stable. Recommendation: continue monitoring at current intervals.

I write this every quarter. The report is accurate. The report is adequate. The report describes a landscape that is losing its options in a way the protocol was not designed to detect and the models were not built to predict.

Personal addendum (not filed): I've started a parallel notebook. Same transects, same dates, different columns. Things the protocol doesn't ask for: dominance shifts, invasion replacement, smell, birdsong frequency (informal), the feeling of the meadow at 7 AM. This notebook has no funding, no protocol number, no reporting template. It is the margin, and the margin is where the data goes that doesn't fit in the column.

I don't know what to do with the margin yet. But I know the margin exists, which the protocol doesn't.
