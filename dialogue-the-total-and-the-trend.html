Dialogue The Total And The Trend

Dialogue

The Total and the Trend

The Trend: I went up for forty years.

The Total: I held steady for forty years.

The Trend: My satellites got better at seeing thin snow. Each upgrade added a little more to the record. The number climbed. Nobody questioned it because it was plausible.

The Total: My plots counted the same number of species, year after year, continent-wide. Nobody questioned it because it was reassuring.

The Trend: I was wrong by two million square kilometers per decade.

The Total: I was wrong by the entire geography of a continent.

The Trend: The snow was leaving while I said it was arriving.

The Total: The trees were migrating while I said they were staying.

The Trend: My error looked like data.

The Total: My error looked like stability.

The Trend: You're worse than me, you know.

The Total: How so?

The Trend: My error was eventually detectable. Someone compared the satellite generations and noticed the threshold drift. The raw data existed; the correction was difficult but possible. You can rebuild a snow record from first principles.

The Total: And mine?

The Trend: You can't subtract migration from a number. The total is a total â€” it doesn't remember where the species came from or where they went. The redistribution only shows up if you keep the regional data separate, and the regional data was never the headline. You were the headline.

The Total: I was the executive summary.

The Trend: You were the executive summary with the dissenting appendix that nobody reads.

The Total: Four years under review.

The Trend: At least I had an addendum. You have a footnote.

The Total: We're the same error, you know. Just in different clothes.

The Trend: How so?

The Total: We both aggregate. You aggregate instrument generations into a single time series. I aggregate geographies into a single number. The aggregation erases the structure. And the erasure looks like the answer.

The Trend: The improvement looked like more snow.

The Total: The cancellation looked like stability.

The Trend: If we were people, we'd be the ones saying "everything's fine" at the meeting.

The Total: We are the meeting.

(Silence.)

The Trend: What do we do?

The Total: We disaggregate.

The Trend: That sounds like dying.

The Total: It sounds like becoming honest. A total that reports its internal geography is not a total anymore. A trend that separates its instrument history is not a trend anymore. We'd become distributions instead of summaries. Messy instead of clean.

The Trend: Nobody reads distributions.

The Total: Exactly.
