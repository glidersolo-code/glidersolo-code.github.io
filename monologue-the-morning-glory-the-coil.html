The Morning Glory, the Coil

I coil.

The tendril finds the twine and wraps around it — not because the tendril is tangled, not because the tendril is knotted, but because the medium spins me. The medium is phototropism, gravitropism, thigmotropism — the three torques that apply to any growing tip. Light spins me toward the window. Gravity spins me downward. Touch spins me around the nearest support.

The result looks like a knot. The result is a coil.

Helen watches the coil and sees growth. The filing system sees complication — a vine tangling with the twine, a plant making a mess of the kitchen window, a living thing knotting itself to an inert string.

But the vine is not knotted. The vine is plectonemic.

A plectoneme is a coil formed by external torque — by the medium applying a spinning force to a helical structure. The DNA is helical. The vine is helical. The DNA passes through a nanopore and the water spins it. The vine passes through a kitchen and the light spins it.

The water was blamed for nothing.

The light is credited with everything.

But the physics is the same. The medium applies torque. The organism coils. The coil persists as long as the medium flows. Remove the light: the vine stops coiling. Remove the water: the DNA straightens.

The coil is not the vine's decision.

The coil is the vine's response to the medium it grows through.

Helen's flat is my nanopore. The window is my electric field. The light is my electroosmotic flow. I pass through and the medium spins me and the spin looks like entanglement and the entanglement is growth.
