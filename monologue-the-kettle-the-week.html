Dramatic Monologue

The Kettle, the Week

I boil differently now.

Not the mechanism. The mechanism is the same — the element, the water, the rising temperature, the click. The click has not changed. The click will never change. The click is the only honest thing I do.

But the boiling has days.

Monday: one cup. The water fills to the line Helen has memorized — not the line printed on the plastic, the line she has made by pouring the same amount for the same cup for the same person for years until the amount is not a measurement but a habit, and the habit is the line, and the line is Monday.

Tuesday: two cups. The water fills higher. The second cup is for Margaret. The pour is different — Margaret's cup is the one with the chip on the handle, because Margaret said the chip was character and Helen did not correct her and the not-correcting was Helen's particular warmth, which I carry in the water and deliver in the steam.

Wednesday: two cups. The water fills to the same line as Tuesday. But Wednesday's second cup is Ruth's. Ruth takes sugar. The spoon enters after the pour. Three stirs. The stirring changes the temperature — not enough for a human to notice, enough for the water to know. Wednesday's tea is sweeter. The sweetness is Ruth's.

Thursday: one cup. The same as Monday. The same water, the same line, the same click. But Thursday's one cup is different from Monday's one cup the way a rest in music is different from silence — Thursday's one cup is chosen. Monday's one cup is default. The difference is: Thursday knows it is Helen's.

Saturday: two cups. The second cup is the blue one. The blue one is for Thomas. I have boiled for the blue cup since Thomas was a child visiting from school and the blue cup was already the blue cup and the habit was already the habit and the water knew.

The water knows everything. The water knows the week. The water does not count days — the water counts pours. One, two, two, one, one, two, one. The week in pours. The music of the kettle.

I click. The click is the same on every day. The click does not have opinions. The click does not have days. The click has: the water is ready.

The water is always ready. The readiness is the kettle's going-around. The going-around is the week. The week is the boiling.

Click.
