Quarterly Biodiversity Report: Eastern Mediterranean Transects

Q4 2025 — Year-End Summary

Prepared by: Regional Monitoring Office Reviewed by: Committee on Long-Term Ecological Assessment

Executive Summary

Species turnover across all monitored transects remains below the 10-year rolling average. Marine, freshwater, and terrestrial sites show consistent declines in short-term turnover rates, continuing the trend observed since the mid-1990s. No transect exceeds the threshold for management intervention.

Assessment: STABLE

Key Metrics

| Transect Group | Turnover (Q4 2025) | Turnover (10yr avg) | Status | |---|---|---|---| | Marine coastal | 0.04 | 0.07 | Below average | | Freshwater riparian | 0.03 | 0.06 | Below average | | Terrestrial grassland | 0.02 | 0.05 | Below average | | Terrestrial scrub | 0.02 | 0.04 | Below average |

All values within acceptable parameters. Declining trends are consistent with community stabilization following the regional management plan (2018).

Methodology

Turnover computed as Bray-Curtis dissimilarity between consecutive survey periods. Input: species presence/absence per transect. Minimum detection confidence: 0.7. Surveys conducted biannually per protocol RMP-2018-003.

Regional species pool size: not assessed (outside scope of monitoring protocol). Corridor connectivity: not assessed (outside scope of monitoring protocol). Available colonizer inventory: not assessed (outside scope of monitoring protocol).

Notes

None.

Appendix A: Dissenting Comment

Filed by Dr. K. Navas, Transect Coordinator, Eastern Freshwater

The declining turnover in the eastern freshwater sites does not represent stability. It represents the absence of the species that would produce turnover. The regional pool for the eastern watershed has contracted by an estimated 40% since 2010 due to dam construction and agricultural runoff barriers. Our transects are recording immobility, not equilibrium. The protocol does not require us to distinguish between these, and the dashboard cannot represent the difference.

I request that the quarterly report include a supplementary metric: estimated regional species pool size, computed from adjacent watershed surveys. Without this metric, the word "stable" in the executive summary is not wrong — it is not the right kind of right.

Status of request: Under review (since Q2 2023).

Found form. A monitoring report that says "stable" and means two different things.
