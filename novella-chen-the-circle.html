Novella Chen The Circle

Poem

Chen, the Circle

He reads the MacNulty comment at his desk, in the banana socks, with the coffee that has gone cold because the paper is four pages long and each page is worse than the last.

Height predicting volume derived from height.

He puts down the paper. He picks up the pen. He writes, on the back of a seminar flyer for a talk he has already missed:

The basis set defines the solution space. If the basis set contains the answer as an input, the calculation will converge to the answer regardless of the physics. This is not an error in the calculation. It is a property of the method. The method cannot fail.

He crosses out the last sentence. He writes:

The method cannot fail. This is the error.

He puts the pen down. He picks up the paper again.

The Yellowstone regression used plant height to compute crown volume. Then it used crown volume to demonstrate the strength of the trophic cascade. The variable measuring the change was derived from the variable being measured. The calculation converged. Of course it converged. It had nowhere else to go.

This is basis set collapse. Not the kind Lena writes about — the kind where the basis set is too small and the answer is wrong because something is missing. This is the other kind: the basis set contains its own answer, and the answer is right — right in the arithmetic, right in the statistics, right at every step — and wrong, because rightness is not the same as truth when the method guarantees the outcome.

He opens his laptop. He writes an email to Maren. Subject line: Read MacNulty et al.

Body:

I think I finally have a name for what happens when the basis set contains the answer. It's not underdetermination (too few constraints) or overdetermination (too many constraints). It's self-determination. The basis determines the solution and the solution was already in the basis. The calculation is a ceremony. The ceremony produces a number. The number looks like a result.

Height → volume → cascade → height.

The circle is invisible because each step is correct.

He sends it. He picks up the coffee. Cold. He drinks it anyway.

On his desk, the flyer for the missed seminar has a title he can see upside down: "New Approaches to Convergence Testing in Density Functional Theory." He turns it over. His own handwriting: The method cannot fail. This is the error.

He puts the flyer in the drawer where he keeps things that are not finished yet. The drawer is getting full.

Novella fragment. Chen reads the Yellowstone paper. Self-determination as the basis set containing its own answer.
