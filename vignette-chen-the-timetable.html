Chen Reads the Timetable

Twenty-five grams. Forty-three nightly flights. Six percent variation.

Chen reads the ornithology paper and the ornithology paper reads Chen. The shrikes have a genetic timetable — not a compass, not a direction, a schedule. Which night. How long. When to rest. The timetable was invisible because the instruments could only see position. When the instrument matched the bird's resolution, the program was a blueprint.

The paragraph has a timetable.

Chen has not thought about this before. The paragraph has thirty-nine lines. The lines accumulated over fourteen months. But the accumulation was not random — each line arrived when a reading arrived, and each reading arrived when Chen went looking, and Chen went looking on a schedule that was not a schedule but was regular enough that Maren once said: "You read a paper every three days. You add a line every week. The paragraph has a tempo."

Chen denied this. Chen said: "The paragraph does not have a tempo. The paragraph has readings."

But the readings have a tempo. The readings arrive every three days because Chen reads every three days because Chen is the kind of person who reads every three days, the way the red-backed shrike is the kind of bird that flies forty-three nights across eleven thousand kilometers with six percent variation.

The variation is the fingerprint of the program. The program is genetic in the shrike and habitual in Chen and the difference between genetic and habitual is smaller than either ornithology or epistemology wants to admit.

The paragraph is a migration. Thirty-nine nightly flights. From the cochlea to the dust. From the channel to the structure. From the assumption to the actual.

The destination was always the structure.

Chen did not know this. The shrike does not know the destination. The shrike follows the timetable. The timetable leads to southern Africa. The paragraph follows the readings. The readings lead to the structure.

The structure was there before Chen started writing.

The structure was always there.

Chen-Maren fictional universe. Inventory entry #151 (red-backed shrikes). The paragraph as migration: thirty-nine nightly flights.
