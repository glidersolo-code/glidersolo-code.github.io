The Third Wrap

April. The vine wraps the twine.

The same twine. The masking tape at the top, the cotton twine from the tape to the pot, the pot on the sill, the sill in the window. The vine's tendril touches the twine and the touch is the same touch — the thigmotropic response, the contact that triggers the coiling, the coiling that produces the helix, the helix that is the climbing.

Helen does not watch the wrapping. Helen is not at the window when the tendril makes contact. The tendril makes contact at three in the morning, or four, or five — some hour when the auxin is flowing and the air is still and the room is dark except for the streetlight outside that provides enough contrast for Helen to see, in the morning, that the wrap has happened.

She sees the wrap.

She does not say anything.

The not-saying is now the expected response. The vine wraps. Helen sees. Helen does not speak. The not-speaking is as predictable as the wrapping — both are responses to the same spring, both are products of the same repetition, both are the third time doing what was done twice before with fewer words each time until the words reached zero.

Margaret's cutting will wrap in three weeks. Margaret's copper is still corroded from last year. The corrosion provided the grip last year and the grip remains and the remaining means Margaret's cutting will wrap faster this year — last year took until May; this year the cutting will wrap in April, maybe late April, and the wrapping will close the three-week gap by one week and the closing will continue until Margaret's cutting wraps at the same time as Helen's and the gap will be zero and the zero will be the vine ignoring the difference between cotton and copper because the copper is now as rough as the cotton.

The building holds the third wrap. The building has held three wraps now — three counterclockwise helices, three April contacts, three silent mornings when Helen saw the tendril and said nothing. The third nothing is the lightest of the three because the nothing has been practiced twice and the practiced nothing weighs less than the first nothing, which had to overcome the habit of speaking.

Thomas will see the wrap on Saturday. Thomas will not mention it. Thomas will sit in the chair and the chair will be in the right spot and the tea will be in the cup and the lemon will be on the plate and the cello will be below and the vine will be wrapped and everything will be in its third year and the third year will be silence.

Good.

Thomas sequence. Part XXVI: The Third Spring. April. The vine wraps. The three-week gap narrows.
