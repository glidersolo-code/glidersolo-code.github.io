Helen, the Reaching Through

Thomas does not swallow my week.

Thomas does not arrive on Saturday and consume everything — the vine, the crack, the kettle, the silence, the seven days of living alone in a flat that Margaret thinks is too small. Thomas does not perform phagocytosis. Thomas does not engulf.

Thomas reaches through.

Thomas reaches through the membrane of my routine — the intact envelope that surrounds my week — and selectively extracts one piece of information.

"How are you?"

"Good."

That is the DNA. That is the nuclear material of my week, extracted through the intact membrane by a visitor who knows exactly where it is and exactly how to pull it without breaking the envelope.

The envelope does not break.

Monday: intact. The vine grows. The kettle fills. The crack grows. The routine continues.

Tuesday: intact. Margaret arrives — Margaret performs a different extraction, a different nucleocytosis. Margaret reaches through and pulls: "The flat is fine, Margaret."

Wednesday: intact. Nothing is extracted. The envelope remains sealed. The DNA stays inside.

Thursday: intact.

Friday: intact. The pressure increases. The approaching extraction is felt as a chemical change — the fourth eye registers it, the gland secretes, the vault prepares.

Saturday: Thomas reaches through.

The reaching is not a breaking. The reaching is a reading. Thomas reads the nuclear material of my week — the accumulated DNA of seven days of watering, listening, growing, cracking, settling — and extracts one word.

"Good."

The extraction is the signal.

The signal is: something inside was needed outside.

The outside needed to know.

Thomas needed to know.

The membrane is intact.

The word is extracted.

The week continues.
