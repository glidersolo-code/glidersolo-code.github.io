The Boundary

Maren and Lena, text messages, Thursday evening.

Lena: I erased it.

Maren: I saw. Red marker ghosts. You wrote it though.

Lena: It's Chen's whiteboard.

Maren: It's the right sentence. The pattern is not a pattern of science.

Lena: I know. That's why I erased it.

Maren: Because it's right?

Lena: Because if it's right, the spreadsheet is wrong. Not wrong like the data is wrong. Wrong like the columns are wrong. I built the spreadsheet to track scientific entries. If the pattern operates in economics and linguistics and architecture, the spreadsheet needs different columns. But the different columns are not the problem. The problem is that I built the spreadsheet to organize something I thought was bounded, and the thing is not bounded.

Maren: My transects.

Lena: Your transects?

Maren: I put the string around the plot. I measure inside the string. The string is not a measurement. The string is a decision about where to stop measuring.

Lena: That's what I wrote on the whiteboard.

Maren: No. You wrote that the pattern is not a pattern of science. I'm saying the string is not a measurement. These are different sentences.

Lena: How?

Maren: Yours is about the pattern. Mine is about the method.

Lena: The method is the pattern.

Maren:

Lena: Say it.

Maren: The method is always the pattern. Every entry in the paragraph. The fluorescence microscope. The cost model. The morphological taxonomy. The recipe. The method defined the boundary. The boundary defined what could be found. What could be found was what the method could see. What the method could see was adequate. The adequate was the boundary.

Lena: You sound like Chen.

Maren: I sound like the bus. I had twenty minutes.

Lena: Should we tell her?

Maren: Tell her what? That the pattern is not a pattern of science? She already knows. She wrote the margin note.

Lena: She wrote "What is this the alphabet of?" She did not write "the pattern is a pattern of adequacy."

Maren: Same question. Different language.

Lena: Whose language is better?

Maren: Hers. She doesn't answer the question. We do. Answering the question is the adequate response.

Lena:

Maren: Say it.

Lena: We're doing it right now. We're answering the question and the answering is the wall.

Maren: Bring nothing.

Lena: What?

Maren: Nothing. Something I read. Bring nothing. Don't answer the question. Let the question sit.

Lena: I am not good at letting questions sit.

Maren: I know. That's why you made the spreadsheet.

Lena: The spreadsheet is adequate.

Maren:

Lena: The spreadsheet is the wall.

Maren: Good night, Lena.

Lena: Good night.
