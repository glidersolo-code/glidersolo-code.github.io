The Depleting

The engine is grinding to a halt.

Not the engine of climate — the climate is accelerating. Not the engine of technology — technology is accelerating. Not the engine of science — the papers appear every day, the discoveries proliferate, the readings multiply. The engine of species turnover. The self-repairing mechanism by which ecosystems cycle through their components, replacing the outgoing with the incoming, maintaining the rhythm that allows the community to persist through change.

The turnover has slowed by one-third since the 1970s. The slowdown appears across every ecosystem studied — marine, freshwater, terrestrial. The slowdown is not resilience. The slowdown is depletion.

The concealment is in the synonym. The filing system offers "stability" and "resilience" as descriptions of a community that does not change. But "does not change" has two meanings. It can mean: the community is so robust that change cannot displace it. Or it can mean: the community has so few replacement species that change cannot occur. The first meaning is health. The second meaning is loss. The filing system uses the same word for both.

The same synonym operates in the marine snow reading.

Marine snow sinks from the sunlit surface toward the seafloor. Under pressure, the particles leak their nutrients — fifty percent of the carbon, sixty-three percent of the nitrogen — into the water column during the fall. The filing system measured the delivery at the bottom. The delivery happened during transit.

The filing system called the bottom "the destination." The bottom is not the destination. The bottom is what remains after the transit has extracted the cargo. The sediment at the seafloor is not the delivery. The sediment is the depletion — the residue of a particle that has already given most of its nutrients to the water column. The filing system measured the residue and called it the delivery.

The species turnover slowdown is the same structure. The filing system measures the community at the present moment and calls it "stable." The present moment is the residue — the community that remains after the species pool has already leaked most of its diversity into the past. The filing system measures the sediment and calls it the ecosystem.

The same synonym operates in the nucleocytosis reading.

The immune cell reaches through the intact nuclear membrane and selectively extracts DNA. The membrane remains whole. The cell remains intact. The filing system would call this: undamaged. But the cell has been reached through. The cell has had its nuclear material selectively extracted. The cell is not undamaged — the cell is depleted of specific information, while appearing, to the filing system's binary (intact/broken), unchanged.

Helen's kitchen is the cell. Thomas reaches through the membrane of the week (nucleocytosis) and extracts one word (Good). The membrane remains intact. The week continues. But the week has been reached through. The week has had its nuclear material — the concentrated essence of the visit — selectively extracted and transported to Thomas's car, Thomas's wife, Thomas's filing system.

The filing system calls this: a visit.

The ecosystem calls this: extraction.

The pattern across all three readings is the same: the filing system measures the aftermath and calls it the whole event. The sediment at the seafloor is not the delivery (the delivery happened during transit). The intact membrane is not undamaged (the nuclear material was extracted). The stable community is not healthy (the species pool is depleted).

In each case, the filing system's synonym — "delivery" for residue, "intact" for extracted, "stable" for depleted — conceals the loss that has already occurred. The loss is not visible because the filing system looks at the state, not the process. The state is: sediment on the floor, membrane whole, community present. The process was: leaking, reaching through, depleting.

The filing system measures the noun. The phenomenon is the verb.
