Monologue Helen The Wolf Tone

Dramatic Monologue

Helen, the Wolf Tone

Ruth wrote back.

Not to the note on blue paper — to the Suite. Ruth wrote a note about the Suite. White paper, folded twice, pushed under my door. She signed it "Ruth (downstairs)" because I signed mine "Helen (upstairs)" and the signing is the location and the location is the relationship and the relationship is: we are two women in the same building separated by two hundred and fourteen millimeters of joist and what the joist carries between us.

The note said: the Suite will be shorter tonight. She is leaving out the Bourrées. The E string has a wolf tone.

I do not know what a wolf tone is. I know "wolf" and I know "tone" and I know that Ruth is leaving out the Bourrées to protect what arrives upstairs, which means Ruth is thinning the Suite the way I thin the morning glory — cutting back what crowds so that what remains has room.

Michael thinned the clematis. Michael thinned with the secateurs, at the base, in March, before the new growth. Michael thinned because the unthinned clematis would reach the roof and the reaching-the-roof was too much and the too-much would damage the guttering and the guttering was the boundary between enough and more-than-enough.

Ruth thins with a bow. Ruth thins by not playing. The not-playing is the thinning. The Bourrées are the wolf tone's territory and Ruth cedes the territory to protect the Sarabande, which is what she sends upstairs, which is what arrives in my kitchen, which is what I hear in the chair, which is: the foundation. The reduced. The essential. The enough.

She is protecting my Sarabande. She is making a choice about what travels through the floor. Before the note, the Suite was the Suite — Ruth played it and the floor carried what the floor could carry and I heard what I heard and the hearing was enough. After the note, Ruth is editing the Suite for the floor. Ruth is the half-inch brush applied to the repertoire.

I will write back. Not immediately. The immediately is Margaret's speed. My speed is: next Tuesday, I will write on blue paper, I will fold it once, I will push it under her door. The note will say something about the wolf tone. I do not yet know what.

Maybe: "I would have heard the wolf tone and thought it was the building."

Maybe: "The wolf tone is welcome."

Maybe: "Thank you."

Maybe: just the Sarabande. The Sarabande tonight, which I will hear differently because I now know Ruth is choosing what to send and what the choosing leaves out is the wolf tone and the leaving-out is the thinning and the thinning is the going-around applied to a string.

The going-around is also a note on white paper. The going-around is also a signature that says "downstairs" because "downstairs" is where the music comes from and "upstairs" is where the music goes and between the two is the floor and the floor carries what the floor can carry and the carrying is enough.

Ruth wrote back. The Sarabande will be the same. The same is the reply.
