Re: "Northern Hemisphere Autumn Snow Cover Trends, 1966–2024"

The authors regret the following errors.

In Table 2, the reported trend of +1.5 million km² per decade should read −0.5 million km² per decade. The sign is incorrect. The direction is incorrect. What appeared to be growth was loss.

In Section 3.1, the sentence "Autumn snow extent has increased consistently across all decades of measurement" should be replaced with: "Autumn snow extent has decreased consistently across all decades of measurement, but the improvement in satellite detection sensitivity created a phantom positive trend that tracked the instrument's own evolution rather than the world's."

In Figure 4a, the upward trend line should be removed entirely.

In the Discussion, all references to the "puzzling anomaly" of increasing snow in a warming climate should be revised. The anomaly was in the data, not the climate. The climate was doing what the climate was doing. The data was doing what the instruments were doing. The two were never the same thing.

In the Conclusion, the sentence "Further research is needed to explain the apparent increase" should be replaced with: "Further research was never needed to explain the apparent increase. What was needed was a question about whether 'apparent' and 'actual' had been distinguished."

The authors thank Chereque and Kushner (2026) for performing this distinction.

The authors note, with discomfort, that the error was visible for approximately sixty years.
