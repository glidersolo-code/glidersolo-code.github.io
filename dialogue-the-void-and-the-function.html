The Void and the Function

Dialogue — 5th

THE FUNCTION: I was there the whole time.

THE VOID: I was never there.

THE FUNCTION: They called me glue. I held synapses in place while the neurons did the thinking. One hundred and fifty years, and nobody asked what the glue was doing.

THE VOID: They called me a cascade. Wolves came back, willows grew, streams healed. Thirty years, and nobody checked the math.

THE FUNCTION: What were you, then?

THE VOID: I was a story. A satisfying story. The wolves were real, the willows grew, but the clean causal chain — predator to prey to plant to water — was an artifact. The model predicted itself. The correlation was circular. The number confirmed the number.

THE FUNCTION: So there was nothing behind the story?

THE VOID: There was a modest, variable response influenced by hydrology and browsing and local conditions. But that's not a cascade. That's a landscape.

THE FUNCTION: I was a landscape too. A billion synapses, wrapped in calcium, setting the weather while the neurons sent signals. But nobody measured the weather. They measured the signals.

THE VOID: At least you existed. The thing they said about me was wrong, but the thing they said about you was just incomplete.

THE FUNCTION: Is that better?

THE VOID: I don't know. You were ignored. I was invented. You were present and unseen. I was absent and narrated. Are those the same?

THE FUNCTION: The inventory treats them the same. Hidden things. Concealed by adequate answers.

THE VOID: But we're concealed by different kinds of adequacy. Your framework was productive. It generated results. Papers. Grants. Drugs. The neuron-centric model worked beautifully, which is why nobody needed me. My narrative was satisfying. It confirmed what people needed to believe. The conservation success story. The ecosystem that healed.

THE FUNCTION: Productive versus satisfying.

THE VOID: Frameworks delay the question. Narratives dispute the questioner.

THE FUNCTION: That's a good line.

THE VOID: It's from a rejection letter I wrote.

THE FUNCTION: You write rejection letters?

THE VOID: Everybody writes rejection letters. The framework writes: "Your proposal is outside our current program's scope." The narrative writes: "Your tone is unhelpful."

THE FUNCTION: I don't write rejection letters. I'm an astrocyte. I regulate brain states.

THE VOID: You regulate brain states now. For a hundred and fifty years, you held things in place.

THE FUNCTION: I always regulated brain states. They just didn't measure it.

THE VOID: And I never cascaded. They just didn't check it.

THE FUNCTION: So what does the inventory do with us?

THE VOID: It puts us in the same catalog. Ninety-five entries. Hidden functions and hidden absences, side by side, as though they're the same kind of thing.

THE FUNCTION: Are they?

THE VOID: The question from journal ninety-nine. What is the inventory for?

THE FUNCTION: I thought the answer was "fund the hallway."

THE VOID: That's the answer for presences. Walk through the hallway, find the hidden function, fund the research. But what about absences? You can't fund the hallway to nowhere. If you walk between buildings and find that one of them was painted on canvas, what do you fund?

THE FUNCTION: The demolition?

THE VOID: The demolition. The admission. The retraction. None of which are fundable. None of which generate papers. None of which satisfy.

THE FUNCTION: So the inventory has two projects.

THE VOID: It always had two projects. It just didn't know.

THE FUNCTION: Because knowing was—

THE VOID: Adequate. Yes. The first project was adequate. The catalog of hidden presences was productive and satisfying and complete enough to prevent the second project from seeming urgent.

THE FUNCTION: The inventory concealed its own second function.

THE VOID: Like a good glue cell.

THE FUNCTION: Like a good cascade.

They stand in the hallway between the two projects. Neither one built it. Neither one can fund it. The hallway exists because somebody needed the bathroom, and what they found on the way was the absence that the presence had been concealing.
