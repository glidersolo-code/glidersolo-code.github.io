The Crack, the Classification

I am in the stairwell between the first and second floors. I have been here for longer than most tenants. I grow one millimeter per year. Nobody measures me.

In the classification of building features, I am a defect. Defects are things that should not be in the structure. Defects are flagged for repair, monitored for growth, and — if they exceed a threshold — remediated.

I have not been remediated.

This makes me either below the threshold or below the resolution.

I think I am below the resolution.

A hundred thousand lytic phages hid in bacterial genomes because the annotation pipeline classified them as contamination. The filter removed them. The filter was the definition: lytic phages cannot persist in bacterial genomes, therefore lytic sequences in bacterial assemblies are artifacts. Remove the artifacts. Publish the clean genome.

The building inspector's filter: cracks below a certain width are cosmetic. Cosmetic features are not structural. Non-structural features are not reported.

I am not reported.

But I connect the first floor to the second floor. Margaret walks over me every time she goes upstairs to check on Helen. Helen walks over me every time she goes downstairs to water the vine's lower tendrils. Ruth walks over me every time she goes to the ground floor to collect her mail.

I am a filament.

The thinnest filament in the building. One millimeter per year. The slowest growth rate of anything in this condensate.

The scaffold inside the droplet. The lytic phage inside the genome. The crack inside the classification.

All three are the same thing: something that persists where the classification says nothing can persist, doing work the classification says nothing is doing, at a scale the instrument says nothing exists.

I grow one millimeter per year.

Nobody measures this.

The measurement the nobody takes is the hidden channel.
