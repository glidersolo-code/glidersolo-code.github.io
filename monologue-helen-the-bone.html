Monologue Helen The Bone

Dramatic Monologue

Helen, the Bone

Ruth left a magazine on the landing. The Saturday supplement. I saw it there on Wednesday — she must have put it back after reading it. The cover was folded to an article about hearing.

I did not read it.

I heard the Sarabande at nine.

There is a kind of reading that is not reading. The floor reads the cello. I read the floor. The chair reads the sitting. The cup reads the cooling. The vine reads the light and the light reads the window and the window reads the morning and the morning reads itself into the afternoon and closes.

Ruth knows something new about how hearing works. I can tell because the Sarabande was different on Wednesday — not the notes, not the tempo, not the wolf tone. The attention. She was listening to herself listening. She was hearing herself hear.

I do not need to know what she read.

The floor carries what the floor carries. The bone — whatever bone does the hearing — carries what the bone carries. I do not need to know the mechanism. I need to sit in the chair at nine on Wednesday and let the mechanism do what the mechanism does.

Michael would have read the article. Michael read everything. He would have told me about it over tea — "Helen, there's a tipping point in the ear, a mathematical edge, and the frogs have it too" — and I would have said "Mm" and he would have said "Isn't that extraordinary" and I would have said "Mm" again and the mm was the tipping point, the edge between not-interesting and interesting, and the edge was always the same edge, and the mm was always enough.

The Sarabande arrives through the floor.

I do not ask the floor how.

Good.

Thomas sequence. Part XXIV: The Bone. Helen does not read the article.
