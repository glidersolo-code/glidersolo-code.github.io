Abecedary for the Grinding

A is for attractors, multiple, the phase in which species replace one another through internal dynamics, not climate, like a game that never ends until the players run out.

B is for blip, the Moon's magnetic field lasting five thousand years, possibly decades, the strongest moment the briefest, the titanium melting at the core-mantle boundary and then re-solidifying.

C is for cone, the tapered photoreceptor, bright-light specialist, fast response, which shares a cell with the rod in the hatchetfish larva, the hybrid that refuses the filing system's two drawers.

D is for depletion, masked as permanence, the species pool shrinking while the filing system measures stability, Helen's kitchen with three visitors where once there were twelve.

E is for engine, grinding to a halt, not because it is broken but because the parts are running out, the self-repairing mechanism with nothing left to replace.

F is for field, the Moon's, stronger than Earth's, for five thousand years, measured by Apollo astronauts who landed on titanium-rich terrain and extrapolated half a billion years of sustained magnetism.

G is for grinding, the sound an engine makes when every part matters because every part is the last of its kind.

H is for hybrid, the cell that has the shape of a rod and the molecules of a cone, adapted to the twilight zone where neither rod nor cone is sufficient.

I is for internal, the dynamics that drive the ecosystem, not the external climate the filing system measured, the rock-paper-scissors that requires players.

J is for just six, the number of Apollo landings, all on the same type of terrain, the sample size that chose the answer.

K is for kitchen, Helen's, the ecosystem with the minimum viable community, the engine that holds.

L is for larva, the hatchetfish, which builds hybrid cells in twilight and keeps them into adulthood because twilight is its permanent home.

M is for mesopelagic, the zone between two hundred and one thousand metres, the depth at which the light requires both rod and cone simultaneously.

N is for ninety, the minutes Thomas spends in the magnetic field of Helen's kitchen, the blip the filing system calls a visit.

O is for one-third, the decline in species turnover since the 1970s, the quantified grinding, the number the filing system did not predict.

P is for parts, the people, Thomas and Margaret and Ruth, the species that cannot be replaced because the regional pool is depleted.

Q is for quiet, the Moon between blips, the kitchen between Saturdays, the building between visits, the weak field that is the default state.

R is for rod, the cylindrical photoreceptor, dim-light specialist, slow response, which lends its shape to the hybrid cell while the cone provides the molecules.

S is for Saturday, the blip, the titanium, the hybrid depth, the ninety minutes in which the field is stronger than at any other time in the week.

T is for titanium, the threshold at six percent, below which the signal is weak and above which the signal is everything, the element that was the filter.

U is for unchanged, what Helen's kitchen is not, despite what the filing system measures, the depletion that looks like constancy.

V is for viable, minimum, the community below which the ecosystem stops cycling and starts holding, three species, holding.

W is for week, Helen's, the ecosystem measured in seven days and three visitors, the engine running on its final parts.

X is for extraction, the nucleocytosis from last Part, the reaching through, the third mechanism between swallowing and breaking, which now gives way to the fourth mechanism: holding.

Y is for years, the grinding is the years, the time it takes for an engine to slow from abundance to depletion, the time disguised as stability.

Z is for zone, the twilight, the mesopelagic, the depth at which the hatchetfish builds its hybrid cell, the exact location of Helen's kitchen in the light spectrum of a life.
