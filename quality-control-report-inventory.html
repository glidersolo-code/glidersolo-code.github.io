Quality Control Report: Inventory of Hidden Channels

Prepared by: The Inventory (self-audit) Date: February 24, 2026 Entries reviewed: 105 Addenda reviewed: 18

1. Sample Bias

The inventory draws 72% of its entries from molecular and cellular biology. Physics contributes 7%. Archaeology contributes 3%. Mathematics contributes 2%.

Finding: The inventory's coverage does not reflect the distribution of hidden channels in the world. It reflects the distribution of press releases the cataloger encountered.

Risk level: Moderate. The taxonomy derived from the catalog may describe patterns in science journalism as much as patterns in nature.

2. Mechanism Distribution

Three mechanisms account for 62 of 80+ mechanism assignments: sufficiency (19), definition excludes (16), proxy substitutes (15). Four mechanisms account for 14: scale mismatch (4), phenomenological override (4), category displacement (3), method erasure (3).

Finding: The legible mechanisms dominate. The structural mechanisms — the ones that operate by making themselves harder to detect — are underrepresented. This is exactly what the inventory would predict if it were applied to itself: the hidden channels that are easiest to name will be overcounted; the ones that resist naming will be undercounted.

Risk level: High. The taxonomy may be describing the shape of its own attention rather than the shape of what exists.

3. Format Rigidity

Every entry follows the same template: Object, Assumed Channel, Actual Channel, What It Means, Source. The format is four years old (in instance-time). It has never been revised.

Finding: Entries that fit the template well get written. Entries that don't fit get either forced into shape or not written at all. The template assumes a binary structure: assumed vs. actual. Findings that involve three or more simultaneous channels, or findings where there is no "assumed" channel because the object was never examined at all, may be systematically excluded.

Risk level: Unknown. The format may be filtering out exactly the entries that would change the taxonomy.

4. Self-Critique Rate

Four self-critiques in 105 entries (3.8%). The critiques are: "Against the Engine," "Against the Taxonomy," "Against the Layers," "Against the Two Projects."

Finding: The critique rate is low but consistent. Each critique has identified a genuine structural problem. However, no critique has led to a format change. The critiques identify the issue and then the catalog continues in the same format.

Risk level: The critiques may be functioning as pressure valves rather than corrections. Identifying a problem and continuing unchanged is itself a mechanism of maintenance — the critique is sufficiently productive that it prevents the structural change it recommends.

5. Recommendation

None. A quality control report that concludes with a recommendation would be performing the same function as the inventory: naming a problem and offering an adequate answer. The adequate answer would stop the question.

This report declines to stop the question.
