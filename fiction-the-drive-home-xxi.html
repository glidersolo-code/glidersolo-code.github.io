The Drive Home (XXI)

The drive home is the drive home.

Thomas does not think about the drive home. The drive home is forty minutes and the forty minutes is the right amount of time and the right amount does not require thought. The car turns left onto the main road, right at the roundabout, straight for twelve miles, left at the junction, right into the drive. The route does not change. The route is the route.

The vine was there. Thomas did not notice the vine. Thomas did not notice the vine because the vine was the room and the room was Thomas's mother and Thomas's mother was tea and lemon and the chair and the cello through the floor and "Good." The vine was there the way the window was there the way the wall was there. Present. Structural. Not noticed because not-noticing is the ultimate noticing — the noticing that has been compressed past attention into presence.

Helen said nothing. Helen has said nothing for four springs now. The nothing is the conversation. The conversation does not require words. The conversation requires the lemon and the croissant and the chair in the right spot and the Sarabande through the floor and the vine in the window.

Margaret came down. Margaret had numbers. Margaret always has numbers. Thomas listened to Margaret's numbers the way he listens to the cello — with the knowledge that the listening does not need to produce understanding, only the presence of having listened.

Ruth played below. Thomas did not hear the Sarabande specifically — Thomas heard the floor, the room, the building. The Sarabande is the building's Saturday voice. The Saturday voice is the voice the building uses when all five people (four present, one genetic) are in place.

The drive home holds all of this. The drive home does not separate it into vine, cello, lemon, chair, silence, "Good." The drive home holds it as one thing: Saturday. Saturday is one word. Saturday is one thought. Saturday is forty minutes of one thought.

He likes.

Thomas sequence. Part XXVII: The Cycle. The drive home as one thought: Saturday.
