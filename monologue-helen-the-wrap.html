Monologue Helen The Wrap

Dramatic Monologue

Helen Speaks: The Wrap

It wrapped.

I knew it would. The knowing is different in the second year. The first year I watched the cutting in the yogurt pot and wondered: will it take? Will it climb? Will it find the twine? The wondering was the first year's engine. The second year's engine is not wondering. The second year's engine is waiting without wondering, which is not patience — patience implies uncertainty — but certainty worn smooth by repetition, the way a handrail is worn smooth by hands.

I touched the twine this morning. Not the wrap — the twine above the wrap. I tested whether the tape was holding. The tape was holding. The masking tape I put up in October, already curling, still holding. The tape does not need to be perfect. The tape needs to be there. The vine does the rest.

Margaret called. I told her: it wrapped. Margaret said: "Already?" I said: "Mm." The "mm" held the "not already — exactly on time, the vine's time, which is not already or late but now." Margaret's cutting has not wrapped. Margaret's wire is copper, from the hardware shop, with a bracket. The bracket is correct. The wire is correct. Everything Margaret does is correct. The vine will wrap when the vine wraps. Margaret's vine will take longer because the copper is smooth and the cotton is rough and the roughness is the invitation and the smoothness is the obstacle and the vine does not know the difference between invitation and obstacle — the vine knows the difference between texture and texture, and the texture it prefers is the one it can grip.

I said "There."

One word. I did not plan one word. The word planned itself. The first year I said "Look at that" and the looking was the year. The second year I said "There it is" and the finding was the year. The second spring I said "There" and the there was everything — the vine, the wrap, the twine, the tape, the glass, the light, the morning, the kettle beginning to boil, the flat that holds all of this and asks for nothing back except the holding.

There.

Thomas sequence. Part XXV: The Second Spring. Helen says one word.
