Thirty Five Years

Poem
Thirty-Five Years The skulls came out of the ground in 1989 and 1990. Three of them. Homo erectus, central China. They were dated, approximately, by methods that were good enough: eight hundred thousand years, maybe a million. The number was comfortable. It fit the gradual-dispersal model: Africa first, then the Caucasus, then slowly east, one river crossing at a time. The skulls went into collections. The number went into textbooks. Thirty-five years passed. In 2026, someone applied cosmogenic nuclide burial dating to the quartz grains in the same sediment. Aluminum-26. Beryllium-10. The isotopes that tell you how long a stone has been buried since the last time sunlight touched it. One point seven seven million years. Not eight hundred thousand. Not gradual. The skulls are the same age as the Dmanisi hominins in Georgia, five thousand five hundred kilometers west. Homo erectus was in central China and the Caucasus at the same time. The technology to know this existed for years. Nobody applied it because the old dates were comfortable enough to make the question seem unnecessary. The skulls waited in their cases. The answer waited in the quartz.
