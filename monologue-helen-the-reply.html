Helen, the Reply

She wrote back.

Not to me. To Ruth. The note on blue paper. I wrote it on Tuesday. I folded it once because folding twice would have been too much â€” the folding twice is for Michael's letters, the letters in the tin, and Ruth is not Michael and the note is not a letter and the Sarabande is not the same thing as the morning glory although sometimes at nine-fifteen when the Suite reaches the Sarabande and the morning glory is dark against the glass and the kitchen is warm because the kettle has just finished, sometimes the Sarabande and the morning glory are exactly the same thing.

I wrote: "The Sarabande was especially good on Thursday. I wanted to say so."

Twelve words. Plus my name. Plus "upstairs" in parentheses because the parentheses are the location and the location is the relationship. I am the woman upstairs. She is the woman who plays. The floor is between us and the floor is also what connects us and the connecting and the separating are the same two hundred and fourteen millimeters.

I should not have written the note. Margaret would say I should have written it sooner. Margaret would be right and also beside the point, which is where Margaret is most comfortable. I wrote it because Thursday was different. Thursday the Sarabande came twice, and between the two Sarabandes there was a silence, and the silence was longer than the usual silence between movements, and the longer silence made the second Sarabande arrive with a different weight. Not better. Not worse. Different. The way Thomas's tea was different last Saturday when he asked for the lemon.

The lemon changed the tea. The silence changed the Sarabande. The note changes the door.

The door between the hallway and Ruth's flat is now a door under which a note has traveled. Before Tuesday it was a door I passed every day. Now it is a door with a gap that carries words. Eight millimeters. The building carries the Sarabande through two hundred and fourteen millimeters of joist. The note traveled through eight millimeters of gap. Both are channels I did not plan. Both carry what I did not expect them to carry.

Thomas wrote back. Six words on ordinary paper. "The lemon was good. Thank you." I put his note in the drawer beside my own letters, which means the drawer now has letters going both ways, which means the drawer is a conversation, which means the sponge and the stamp and the lemon are all part of the same thing.

Ruth has not written back. The Sarabande tonight was the same Sarabande. I think the same Sarabande is the reply.

I think the same is enough. The same has always been enough. Michael knew this. The fence needed painting every spring. The painting was the same. The morning glory was different every spring but the going-around was the same: the half-inch brush, the careful edge, the leaf you do not paint.

The same is the reply.
