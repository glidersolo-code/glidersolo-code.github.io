Ruth Speaks: The Tension

The bow creates negative pressure.

I mean this acoustically. The bow pulls across the string. The string vibrates. The vibration creates regions of compression and rarefaction in the air — positive and negative pressure, alternating, propagating outward as sound. The Sarabande is made of alternating positive and negative pressure.

But the bow's action is a pull. Not a push. The cellist does not push sound into the room. The cellist pulls the string, and the string pulls the air, and the air carries the alternation to whoever is listening.

Helen is listening through the floor. Helen has been listening for three years. Helen hears the negative pressure — the rarefaction, the thinning of air, the moment between notes where the silence is slightly less than silence — and the negative pressure is the part Helen hears best, because the floor filters out the positive pressure and leaves the rest.

The rest is the pulling.

The Sarabande has a pause in the fourth measure. The pause is pure negative pressure — the moment when the bow lifts, the string stops, the air settles, and the only thing left is the pulling: the listener pulling toward the next note, the note pulling toward the listener, the floor pulling the vibration downward, the silence pulling upward.

The pause is the best thing I play.

The pause is the thing I learned in Bergen when my teacher said: the Third Suite is the one you play when you have forgiven yourself for not being able to play it. The forgiveness is in the pause. The forgiveness is the negative pressure — the moment of release, of letting go, of pulling rather than pushing.

Helen hears the pause.

Helen hears the pulling.

Helen hears the forgiveness.

The floor carries all of this. The floor does not know it carries forgiveness. The floor carries vibrations at frequencies determined by my bow speed and my finger position and the diameter of my C string. The floor does not know about forgiveness any more than the xylem knows about signals.

The xylem carries the tension. The floor carries the forgiveness.

Neither knows what it carries. Both carry it anyway.

Thomas sequence. Ruth on the bow as negative pressure. The pause is the best thing she plays. Helen hears the pulling through the floor.
