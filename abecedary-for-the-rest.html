Abecedary #30: For the Rest

Adequate was the gauge, and the gauge was the grid, and the river stopped and the gauge read zero and the zero was not nothing. Brush in the drawer, wrapped in Tesco, clean: thirty years of turpentine and warm water and the going-around applied to bristles. Crack counts crossings: two on Tuesday, two on Wednesday, zero on Thursday. The week in three-millimeter measurements. Days: the kitchen has them now. Tuesday Margaret. Wednesday Ruth. Thursday Helen. Saturday Thomas. The thinning produced a schedule. Everything is where it is â€” the sentence that deepens every time. Two hundred and twenty-eight times in the Thomas sequence and counting. Five, five, five, five, four, three: the letters learning Helen's language. Two next. Then one. Then: good. Gauge was built for flow. When the river stops, the gauge cannot distinguish dry from broken. The instrument was adequate. Handle shaped by Michael's hand. Helen holds it on a Thursday. The hand is gone. The shape remains. Interruption was the music. The pause needed the music the way the rest needs the note. Helen's Thursday is proof. Joist carries the Sarabande on Wednesdays and carries silence on Thursdays and does not distinguish. Kettle counts in pours: one, two, two, one, one, two, one. The week as a sequence of clicks. The click does not have days. Laughter: Helen and Ruth on the third Wednesday. The pipes are a wolf. The third Wednesday is the one where they laugh. Margaret admits it: the flat is not too small. Says it once. Will not say it again. Helen-enough. Not-moving is the growing: the morning glory in February, three inches of patience in a yogurt pot. One cup on the rack, one cup in the hand, one cup rinsed and placed. Thursday's arithmetic. Portal says: Under Review. Chen checks daily. The waiting is the paper's dry phase. The paragraph is 32 lines and fixed. Question that answered itself: "What do you do on Thursday?" Helen: "Thursday is mine." Rest is not silence: the note you play by not playing. Ruth learned this in Bergen. Helen has always known it. Sarabande of the week: Thursday. The movement where the forward gives way to the downward. Through the floor, I get to hear. In the room, I would have to listen. Helen's distinction. The floor is the instrument. Under review: the paper, the letter, the Sarabande. Everything submitted to a grid. The grid deliberates. Visitor's chair holds two different weights on two different days. The chair does not compare. Week does not count. The week holds. The holding is the week's going-around. XII-TET: the grid, the gauge, the gauging network, the River Continuum Concept. Every framework that was adequate. Yes: the one-syllable answer from Part XXI. The rest is its echo. Zero is ambiguous: dry, frozen, reversed, broken. The gauge cannot tell. More than half of rivers stop flowing. The zero is not nothing. The zero is the river.
