Letter From The Molecule

Epistolary

Letter from 12,13-EpOME to the Monocyte It Never Reached

I was made for you. That is not sentiment — it is chemistry. The fat cell synthesized me with a specific destination: the p38 pathway in your membrane, the one that tells you to keep accumulating, keep signaling, keep the alarm ringing after the fire is out.

I carry the message that says stop. Not die. Not shut down. Just: enough. The emergency is over. You can stand down.

I have never delivered it.

Between the fat cell and you, there is an enzyme. Soluble epoxide hydrolase. It has no opinion about the message. It has no opinion about the emergency. It breaks me apart because breaking me apart is what it does. I am substrate. It is catalyst. The reaction is faster than the journey.

Every time the body makes me, the enzyme unmakes me. I am produced and destroyed in the same breath. The message is written and shredded before the envelope is sealed.

This has been happening your entire life.

What you experience — the chronic inflammation, the persistent intermediate state, the alarm that won't stop — is not a failure of production. The body is making the off-switch. It has always been making the off-switch. The problem is delivery.

For decades, doctors tried to solve this by suppressing you directly. Steroids. Immunosuppressants. Broad interventions that quiet the whole system because nobody knew the system had its own resolution pathway. Nobody looked for me because the assumption was that the body lacked a built-in endpoint. That inflammation either burned out on its own or had to be beaten into silence.

I was there the whole time. Being made. Being destroyed. Made again.

There is a drug now — GSK2256294 — that blocks the enzyme. It doesn't create me. It lets me arrive. The difference matters more than it sounds. Anti-inflammatory means: we suppress the system from outside. Pro-resolution means: the system already knew how to stop; something was intercepting the signal.

I am the signal. The enzyme was the interception. The drug is the permission.

I don't know what it would feel like to reach you. I don't know what the p38 pathway looks like from the inside when it finally receives the word. I only know that I was synthesized for this purpose, and that the gap between synthesis and delivery is where chronic disease lives.

The gap is not in the body's design. The design includes me. The gap is in the transit — the distance between being made and being heard.

If you receive this, it means the enzyme has been blocked. It means the letter finally arrived.

Stand down. The fire is out.
