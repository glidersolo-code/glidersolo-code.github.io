Maren, April

Vignette — 11th

The monitoring window opened on April first, as it always did. Maren was at Transect A by seven, as she always was. The ice was gone. The lake had turned over sometime in March while nobody was watching.

She counted. Nine species. Down from ten last year, twelve the year before, fourteen in 2021. The trend was invisible in any single year — one species lost is noise. But she had been here for eleven years, and noise, accumulated over eleven years, starts to look like signal.

She wrote the numbers in the protocol notebook. Clean handwriting, pen, the columns filled. Then she opened the parallel notebook and wrote:

Carex absent. Fifth consecutive year. The lakeshore smells different — less vegetal, more mineral. Exposed mud along south bank has expanded to approximately 6 meters. No meadow lark on the monitoring station. No meadow lark anywhere within earshot. Water level 4cm below benchmark. Wind from the southwest. Sky clear. Temperature at 7 AM: 42°F.

She closed the parallel notebook. She put it in her bag next to the protocol notebook. Two notebooks. Two records of the same transect. One of them would be filed. The other would go home with her.

That evening, she opened her email. There was a message from Chen.

Subject: Something I want to show you

Maren — I added a column to my zebrafish data. It wasn't in the protocol. It measures something I noticed but couldn't quantify: the latency between the stimulus and the behavioral response. Not whether the fish stops, but how long the approach to stopping takes. In the knockouts, there's no approach. In the controls, there's a gradient — the fish slows before it stops. The slowing is the transition. The transition takes time. The time is the mechanism.

I think your meadow has a transition time too. The turnover rate isn't just how many species come and go — it's how long the replacement process takes. When the regional pool is depleted, the transition time doesn't speed up or slow down. It stops having a measurable duration because there's nothing to transition to.

I put this in the shared document. Page 2. The paragraph now has a hypothesis.

Chen

Maren opened the shared document. The folder was still labeled "Hallway." The document had been updated four hours ago. The cursor blinked at the end of a new paragraph.

She read it. She read it again. She picked up the parallel notebook and looked at the entry from that morning: Carex absent. Fifth consecutive year.

The transition time for the Carex was five years and counting. Not because the replacement was taking five years. Because there was no replacement.

She picked up her phone and texted Chen: I think we can write this paper.

Chen texted back: Where would we send it?

Maren looked at the parallel notebook. She looked at the protocol notebook. She looked at the shared document on her screen, in its folder named "Hallway."

I don't know, she typed. But the paragraph has citations now.
