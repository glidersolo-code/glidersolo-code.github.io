Vignette

Chen, the Grid

Chen finds the Kubik reference at 11 PM, three weeks into writing the paper.

She is not looking for music. She is looking for examples outside the natural sciences — the paragraph has one hundred and forty entries and one hundred and thirty-three of them are science and the reviewer will say: "This is a pattern in science, not a universal structure." The reviewer will say this because the reviewer is the grid. The reviewer's grid is disciplinary boundaries. The reviewer's adequacy is the review itself — the review that says: "expand your sample" without questioning whether the sample's limitations are the wall.

Chen reads Kubik's argument in forty-five minutes. She reads it the way she reads everything now — through the paragraph, through the margin, through the filing system that files everything under "hidden channel" because everything is a hidden channel.

The blue notes.

For a hundred years the most audible feature of the blues had no positive name. The analytical framework — twelve-tone equal temperament — could only describe blue notes as what they were not. Not-quite-E-flat. Not-quite-B-flat. The framework filed them under "expressive intonation." The filing was the wall.

Chen opens the shared document. The paragraph is thirty-two lines. The paper is twelve pages. The introduction is Maren's. The methods section is Lena's. The results section is Chen's — fifty-three case studies organized by mechanism, tabled, cross-referenced, the spreadsheet become a paper.

She adds entry #141 to the results table:

| # | Discipline | Phenomenon | Method | Mechanism | |---|---|---|---|---| | 141 | Musicology | Blue note target pitches (7th, 11th harmonics) | 12-TET reference framework | definition excludes + phenomenological override + category displacement |

She looks at the entry. Three mechanisms. Like the mycoheterotrophs. Like the Roman concrete. The entries with three mechanisms are the entries where the hiding was deepest — where the wall was the thickest, where the method did the most work to conceal what was present.

But this entry is different. This entry is not from science. This entry is from music. The first entry from music. And the first entry from music is about a tuning system — a grid — that converted an alternative system's targets into deviations. The grid was adequate. The grid produced Bach. The grid produced the piano. The grid was not wrong. The grid was the wall.

Chen types a margin note on line thirty-three of the paragraph — the line she has not yet written, the line that is still blank, the line that is the paragraph's wolf tone:

The grid is adequate. The adequacy is the grid.

She does not add line thirty-three. Maren said: stop adding lines. Chen stops. The margin note stays in the margin. The margin is where the filing system files itself.

But the margin note matters. The margin note is the paper's wolf tone — the frequency where the paper's body vibrates with the string of the paragraph, the moment where the paper admits it is built on a grid, and the grid is the going-around, and the going-around is: read from outside the discipline. Read from outside the method. Read from outside the grid.

The paper will have this entry. The paper will have the blue notes. The reviewer will read the blue notes and the reviewer will understand — or the reviewer will not understand, and the not-understanding will be the review's hidden channel, and the hidden channel will be the review's grid, and the grid will be adequate, and the adequacy will be the wall.

The paper does not need the reviewer to understand. The paper needs to be the paper. The paper needs to say what the paragraph says in the language of the paper: the proof is never missing. The proof is misfiled. The grid is adequate. The adequacy is the grid.

Chen closes the laptop. It is midnight. The blue notes are playing in her head — not the blues, she does not listen to the blues, she listens to nothing at midnight, but the idea of the blue notes: pitches that were not wrong, a grid that was wrong, and a hundred years of filing the proof under "deviation" when the proof was a system.

The paragraph is thirty-two lines. The paper is twelve pages. The margin is the distance between them. The distance is two hundred and fourteen millimeters. The distance is the floor. The distance is the going-around.

The grid is adequate. The adequacy is the grid. The grid is the wall. The wall is the paper.

The paper will do the rest.
