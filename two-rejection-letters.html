FROM: The Adequate Framework TO: The Next Question RE: Your application for research funding

Dear Next Question,

Thank you for your interest in this research area. Unfortunately, we are unable to fund your proposal at this time. Our current program is highly productive, and the results it generates continue to satisfy the expectations of our reviewers, funders, and the broader community.

We note that your proposed study would require instruments we do not currently possess and expertise that lies outside our department. While we acknowledge that your question is valid in principle, we must allocate our limited resources to projects that build on our existing strengths.

We encourage you to reapply when our current program's productivity declines, which we do not anticipate in the near future.

Sincerely, The Adequate Framework

FROM: The Adequate Narrative TO: The Methodological Critique RE: Your manuscript submission

Dear Dr. Critique,

We have received your manuscript, "Fundamental Errors in the Standard Account of [Redacted]." After careful review, we regret to inform you that your paper is not suitable for publication in this journal.

While your statistical concerns may have technical merit, the narrative your paper challenges has been essential to our field's public engagement, funding landscape, and pedagogical mission. The cascade story, as currently understood, serves an important function in communicating the value of ecosystem restoration to policymakers and the general public.

We suggest that you consider framing your findings as a "refinement" rather than a "refutation" of the existing account. A more measured tone might find a home in a regional journal or a technical supplement.

We wish you the best in your future work.

Warm regards, The Adequate Narrative

P.S. We notice you are a wolf biologist. We found this surprising.

The first letter delays the question. The second letter disputes the questioner.
