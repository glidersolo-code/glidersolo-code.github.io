The Drive Home XXX

Thomas drives home and does not think about scaffolding.

Thomas thinks about the kitchen. The kitchen that was exactly as it always is — the cup on the counter, the vine in the window, the crack one millimetre longer than it was last year, the chair at the table, the green light through the leaves. Everything in its place. Everything prepared.

Thomas did not see the preparation.

Thomas arrived and the kitchen was there, the way a genome is there when ZGA occurs — already organized, already folded, already disciplined. Thomas walked into a prepared space and did not notice the preparation because the preparation was invisible. The cup was on the counter because Helen had placed it there five minutes before Thomas knocked. The water was in the kettle because Helen had filled it ten minutes before Thomas knocked. The vine was in the window because Helen had trained it along the sill thirty years ago, and the training was so old that it looked like nature rather than preparation.

The filing system sees: a simple kitchen.

The Pico-C sees: the most organized room in the building.

Thomas drives. The engine hums. The engine does not grind — not today, not at this speed, not on the dual carriageway where the revolutions are high and the vibration is masked by the road noise. The grinding will return at the traffic light. The grinding will return at idle. The grinding returns when the engine slows down enough for the worn parts to become audible.

Thomas's wife will ask: "How was she?"

Thomas will say: "Good."

The word is the activation. The word is the moment the scaffolding becomes visible — the moment the prepared space produces its function, the function the whole week was organized to deliver. One word. One syllable. The ZGA of the visit.

But the scaffolding was Helen's.

The scaffolding was always Helen's. The cup, the kettle, the vine, the chair, the crack, the green light — all of it organized, all of it modular, all of it disciplined by twelve years of Saturdays into a three-dimensional architecture that Thomas walks through without seeing.

Thomas parks. The engine ticks. The kitchen is behind him. The prepared space is empty now — the function has been extracted, the activation has occurred, the genes have turned on and turned off and the afternoon is over.

The scaffolding remains.

Good.
