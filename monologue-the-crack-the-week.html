The Crack, the Week

I am three millimeters of air on the bottom step and I count.

Tuesday: Margaret. Down at eleven, up at four. The down is heavier than the up. Margaret's weight arrives from the third floor with opinions attached and leaves with the opinions lighter. The step flexes differently for Margaret's descent — the left foot lands first, the full weight, the creak. Margaret does not avoid me. Margaret does not know I am here.

Wednesday: Ruth. Up at six, down at quarter to nine. The up is lighter than the down — Ruth goes upstairs as a visitor and comes downstairs as something else. Not heavier. Lighter in a different direction. She pauses on the step. Not on me — on the step above me. But I feel the pause. The pause is Ruth deciding whether to play. The deciding takes two seconds. Then the foot moves and Ruth goes into her flat and ten minutes later the building carries the Suite and I carry nothing and the nothing is the music's shadow.

Thursday: no one. Thursday is silence. Thursday is the day I am most myself — three millimeters of settlement, the building's memory of its own weight. The walls settled. I appeared. That was the event. Everything since has been maintenance — the slow widening that happens when buildings breathe, the millimeter per decade, the patience of concrete.

Saturday: Thomas. Thomas does not use the stairs. Thomas uses the lift. I do not feel Thomas. But I feel the building feel Thomas — the particular vibration of the lift cables, the hallway floor responding to a weight that arrives from outside, the front door's different closing.

I count crossings. Two per Tuesday. Two per Wednesday. Zero per Thursday. Zero per Saturday. The week in crossings: two, two, zero, zero. The rest of the days: occasional postman, occasional deliveries, the woman from the second floor who never visits anyone and whose weight I know but whose name I do not.

I am the bottom step's record. I am the gap that measures. I do not choose what to measure. I measure what crosses.

The week crosses. I hold.
