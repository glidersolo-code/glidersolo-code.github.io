Chen, the Dashboard

Chen reads the Nwankwo and Rossberg paper at his desk in the fish room.

This is where he reads everything now — not in his office, which has been painted and smells of primer, but in the basement where Tank 14 hums and the knockout fish circles and the fluorescent lights give everything the same flat color. The fish room is not comfortable. It is consistent. Chen prefers consistency to comfort, which is why he is a neuroscientist and not a poet.

The paper says: species turnover declining despite accelerating climate. The metric says stable. The species pool says depleted.

He looks at Tank 14. The fish circles. The behavioral assay says: active. The knockout — the removal of the astrocytic calcium channels — says: the fish cannot transition between behavioral states. It cannot stop swimming. It looks active. It is stuck.

The dashboard says active. The species pool says depleted. The fish says swimming. The turnover metric says stable.

All four are producing output that looks like a positive condition and describes a negative one. The instruments are functioning. The instruments are not lying. The instruments cannot represent the distinction between genuine activity and the inability to stop.

He opens the paragraph — the running document in the shared folder, the one with Maren's entries and Lena's notes and his own line about the sufficiency being the lock. He types:

Entry 118 (Maren's filing, her numbering). The dashboard and the fish tank. The turnover metric produces stable when the system is equilibrated. The turnover metric also produces stable when the system is depleted. The behavioral assay produces active when the fish is swimming freely. The behavioral assay also produces active when the fish cannot stop. Both instruments have a floor where their output becomes ambiguous. The floor is the most dangerous value because it is the value at which the instrument stops being informative and starts being reassuring.

He reads it back. It is too long. He cuts the last sentence and the entry is better. He is learning, from Maren, that cutting is how you find the sentence that was there all along.

Chen-Maren novella. Chen connects entry #118 to Tank 14. The dashboard and the fish: both producing output that cannot represent being stuck.
