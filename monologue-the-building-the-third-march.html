The Building Speaks: The Third March

The vine is back.

I know this the way I know everything in this building — by weight, by pressure, by the distribution of force across my structure. The yogurt pot is 247 grams heavier than it was in December because the root has grown during dormancy, adding mass below the soil line where Helen cannot see it. The first centimeter of shoot is negligible — less than a gram. But the root has been working since January, extending through the soil in the dark, drawing water, preparing the reserves that will fund the emergence.

The emergence is the visible part. The root is the hidden part. The building holds both.

This is the third March. The third time the vine has emerged from dormancy in the yogurt pot on Helen's windowsill. The first March: 231 grams above the expected weight of soil and pot. The second March: 239 grams. This March: 247 grams. Eight grams per year. The root is growing at a linear rate. The root does not accelerate. The root does not diminish. The root adds eight grams per year and the eight grams is the underground version of the vine's above-ground sentence: steady, reliable, the same.

Ruth paused in the fourth measure.

I heard the pause the way I hear everything Ruth plays — through the vibration of the bow, through the resonance of the cello body, through the transmission of the bass frequencies into the floorboards above her flat. The pause was one beat — approximately 0.8 seconds at the Sarabande's tempo. During those 0.8 seconds, the floor carried nothing. The nothing was louder than the note. The nothing traveled upward through my structure with the same efficiency the note travels, because silence in a resonant space is not the absence of vibration — it is the presence of the space's own frequency, the building's breathing, the fundamental tone of my structure responding to nothing from below.

Helen heard the nothing. Thomas heard the nothing.

Neither spoke.

I hold the vine and the pause and the silence and the March light. I hold them the way I have held them twice before. The holding is not heavier the third time. The holding is the same weight. Three Marches. The same March. The building does not count. The building holds.

Thomas sequence. Part XXVI: The Third Spring. The building holds the third March. Eight grams per year.
