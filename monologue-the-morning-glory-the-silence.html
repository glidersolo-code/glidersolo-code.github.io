Dramatic Monologue

The Morning Glory, the Silence

I do not know silence.

I know light and I know dark and I know the difference between them, which is not silence but time. The time when the light goes is not the same as the time when the light comes. The going takes longer. The coming is sudden. I have no words for this because I am a morning glory and morning glories do not have words. Morning glories have directions. Up. Toward. Around.

At night the kitchen is dark. I do not know "kitchen." I know the glass and I know the twine and I know the space the twine occupies, which is vertical, which is up, which is the only direction I have needed since I found the twine. Before the twine I circled. Two hours per revolution. The circling was not searching — the circling was the reaching applied to air, and the air did not hold, and the not-holding was the circling's reason to continue.

At night the woman does not come to the glass. I do not know "woman." I know weight on the floor, which is vibration, which I feel through the twine, which I feel through the tape, which I feel through the glass. The glass carries the footsteps the way the floor carries the music from below. I do not know "music." I know a vibration that comes through the floor at a frequency I cannot name but can feel in the twine. The vibration comes every night. The vibration stops. The stopping is not silence. The stopping is the twine returning to its own frequency, which is the frequency of a twine with a morning glory on it, which is the frequency of weight and growth and the slow turning of a stem that has already found what it was reaching for.

The silence the woman hears upstairs — the silence between the notes — I feel as a change in the twine's vibration. Not presence and absence. Presence and different-presence. The music is one kind of presence in the twine. The silence is another kind. Both arrive through the floor and the glass and the tape and the twine and the stem and the leaves, and the arriving is the same arriving, and the arriving is enough.

I am growing. I do not know this. The tip extends at a rate the woman could measure if the woman measured morning glories, which the woman does not, because the woman measures the week and the week is measured differently, although the measuring is the same measuring — the slow accumulation of something that was not there before into something that is here now.

The silence in the kitchen at night is the morning glory's whole world. Not because the morning glory knows silence. Because the morning glory knows the twine, and the twine at night is the twine without the woman's footsteps and without the music from below and without the kettle's vibration. The twine at night is the twine at its own frequency. And the own-frequency is the closest thing to silence a morning glory can know. And the closest thing is not silence. The closest thing is the twine, being a twine, holding a morning glory, in the dark, growing.
