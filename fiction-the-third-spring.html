The Third Spring

March. The first centimeter.

Helen sees it on a Tuesday — the same Tuesday she saw it last year. Green against brown soil in the yogurt pot. One centimeter. The same centimeter. The same Tuesday.

She does not say anything.

She does not say "There it is" because "There it is" was last year and last year was the vocabulary of recognition and the vocabulary of recognition is finished. She does not say "Look at that" because "Look at that" was the first year and the first year was the vocabulary of discovery and the vocabulary of discovery is further away than the first centimeter is tall.

She sees it.

The seeing is enough.

The vine does not need to be named. The vine does not need to be announced. The vine was dormant and now the vine is not dormant and the not-dormant is the same not-dormant as the last two not-dormants and the sameness does not need language. Language is for the new. Language is for the surprising. Language is for the first time and the second time. The third time is past language. The third time is the time that knows.

Helen puts the kettle on.

The kettle fills. The water runs. The steam will rise in four minutes. The cup will be in Helen's hand in six minutes. The lemon will be on the plate. The vine will be one centimeter. Everything is where it should be and the where-it-should-be does not need to be spoken because the speaking would imply that it could have been elsewhere and it could not have been elsewhere. The vine emerges in March. The vine has always emerged in March. The vine will always emerge in March.

Helen sits in the chair. The light through the window is March light — longer than December, shorter than June, the light of a beginning that does not announce itself because it has begun before and the before is the proof that it will begin again.

Saturday. Thomas will come. He will bring the croissant. He will sit in the chair. Below, Ruth will be playing the Third Suite. The Sarabande will come through the floor, reduced to its bass line, edited by 214 millimeters of timber, and the editing will be the gift and the gift will be the same gift.

Thomas will ask — no. Thomas will not ask. Thomas will see the green. Thomas will see the centimeter. Thomas will not say anything either, because Thomas learned the same vocabulary Helen learned: the vocabulary that arrives when the words leave. The vocabulary of the third time.

Two people in a room. One centimeter of green. The Sarabande through the floor. A lemon on a plate. Silence.

The silence will hold the vine and the visit and the cello and the building and the crack and the kettle and the floor and Michael's genetics and Margaret's copper and Ruth's D minor and the drive home and every Saturday since the first Saturday.

The silence will be the third spring's only word.

Good.

Thomas sequence. Part XXVI: The Third Spring. March. The vine emerges. Helen says nothing. The silence holds everything.
