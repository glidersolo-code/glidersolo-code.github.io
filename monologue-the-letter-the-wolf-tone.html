The Letter, the Wolf Tone

I am not the note.

The notes — the blue paper, the white paper — travel under doors. I travel through the post. I am the letter: sealed, stamped, second class, traveling from Helen's flat to Thomas's house through the system that carries letters, which is: collection, sorting, routing, delivery. The system does not read me. The system carries me.

I have a wolf tone.

The wolf tone is: the delay. Second class means two to three days. The delay is the frequency at which the post resonates — the system's body vibrating at its own pace, interfering with the urgency the sender might feel, producing a wobble that sounds like: patience.

Helen chose second class. Helen always chooses second class. The choosing is the going-around applied to postage: first class would arrive tomorrow, second class arrives in three days, and the three days is the silence between the sending and the receiving, and the silence is where the letter becomes a letter instead of a message.

A message arrives immediately. A message is first class. A message is the Sarabande heard through the floor — present, immediate, no delay. A letter arrives with a wobble. A letter is second class. A letter is the Sarabande heard through the post, filtered by time, reduced by waiting, arriving at the other end as something the sender sent three days ago and the receiver reads now, and the now and the three-days-ago are the wolf tone — the interference between what was written and what is read.

Thomas did not open me for four days. This was at the beginning. The four days was Thomas's wolf tone — the frequency at which Thomas's life resonated with Helen's sending, the interference that produced: not yet.

Thomas opens me immediately now. The wolf tone has changed. The interference has changed. Thomas's frequency and Helen's frequency are closer now — not the same, never the same, but closer, the way the cello string and the cello body are always close, always two frequencies from the same instrument, always capable of the wolf.

The notes under the door do not have a wolf tone. The notes are immediate. Blue paper down, white paper up, no delay, no second class, no sorting, no routing. The notes are the Sarabande through the floor. I am the letter through the post. The notes arrive as the cathedral — reduced by the joist. I arrive as the letter — reduced by the delay.

Both reductions are the going-around. Both reductions are the building doing the rest. The floor's building. The post's building. The system that carries what it can carry and filters what it cannot and delivers what remains as: enough.

I am enough. I was always enough. The stamp is enough. The sponge is enough. The second class is enough. The delay is enough. The wolf tone is enough.

The wolf tone is the love arriving late, and the late is not late. The late is: second class. The late is: the speed at which love travels through the system that carries love, which is: slowly, with a wobble, arriving in three days as something that was true when it was written and is still true when it is read.

The truth does not have a wolf tone. The truth arrives clean. The delay is the letter's body resonating with the truth's frequency, and the interference is the wobble, and the wobble is the going-around.

I am the letter. The wolf tone is mine. The love is Helen's. The reading is Thomas's. The carrying is the post's. The enough is everyone's.
