After the Conference

Epistolary — 6th

From: w.chen@jhu.edu To: m.kowalski@state.edu Subject: The hotel bar conversation Date: March 3, 2026

Maren,

I've been thinking about our conversation in Chicago. You said your ecosystem "looks stable because the mechanism that would cause change is gone." I've been writing that sentence on the whiteboard in my office for two weeks and my postdoc thinks I've lost it.

The fish I study has the same structure. The knockout removes the calcium signal that drives behavioral transitions. Without it, the fish just continues. It doesn't distress. It doesn't struggle. It swims. And the behavioral assay we use — number of movements per minute — shows "normal activity." The fish looks fine.

Your monitoring station says the community is stable. My behavioral assay says the fish is active. Both measurements are accurate. Both measurements are missing what stopped working.

I don't know what to do with this. It's not publishable. There's no journal for "a neuroscientist and an ecologist noticed a structural parallel in a hotel bar." But I keep thinking about it.

Also: my grandmother had frontotemporal dementia. I shouldn't have told you that at a conference. But I think the reason I study transitions — the mechanisms that let an animal decide to stop — is because I watched someone lose the mechanism for finishing a sentence. The fish doesn't stop. My grandmother stopped too early. Both are missing the same kind of thing.

Chen

From: m.kowalski@state.edu To: w.chen@jhu.edu Subject: RE: The hotel bar conversation Date: March 5, 2026

Chen,

I started a parallel notebook. Same transects, same dates, but columns for things the protocol doesn't ask for. Dominance shifts. Invasion replacement. Birdsong frequency (informal). The smell of the lake.

I don't know what to do with it either. There's no line item on the grant for "things the ecologist notices that don't fit the model." But I've been counting birds in the margins for three years now, in pencil, and the pencil data are showing something the official data don't: the specialists are being replaced by generalists, one by one, in a way that the species count holds steady while the community loses its character.

Character. That's not a scientific word. My advisor would say: "Define your terms." But I think character is what your fish is missing. Not activity — character. The activity continues. The transitions don't.

I'm sorry about your grandmother. I think you should keep telling people about her, even at conferences. The science comes from somewhere. The somewhere matters.

Maren

P.S. The meadow lark is still gone.

From: w.chen@jhu.edu To: m.kowalski@state.edu Subject: RE: RE: The hotel bar conversation Date: March 7, 2026

Your parallel notebook is the hallway. You know that, right?

Chen
