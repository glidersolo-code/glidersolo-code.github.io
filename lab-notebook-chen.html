Lab Notebook Chen

Poem

Lab Notebook: Chen, W.

Found form: research notebook entry

Date: Tuesday Project: Astrocytic calcium dynamics in behavioral state transitions Subject: 14-7 (zebrafish, Gfap-GCaMP6s × Cx43-KO) Trial #: 47

Protocol: Standard inescapable-flow paradigm. Virtual walls active. Flow rate 2.3 cm/s. Temperature 28.1°C. Ambient light 300 lux. Recording started 21:02.

Observations:

21:02 — Subject placed in arena. Immediate orientation to flow. Tail beat frequency 4.2 Hz.

21:15 — Continued swimming. TBF stable at 4.1 Hz. No change in burst frequency. Calcium imaging shows baseline fluorescence in astrocytic populations. No accumulation.

21:45 — Continued swimming. TBF 4.0 Hz. Controls (wildtype, tank 13) showed onset of intermittent pausing at ~22 min. Subject 14-7 shows no pausing.

22:30 — Continued swimming. TBF 3.9 Hz (slight decrease attributable to metabolic drift, not behavioral transition). Controls entered persistent quiescence at ~38 min. Subject 14-7 shows no state change. Astrocytic calcium: flat. No accumulation curve visible in any ROI.

23:00 — Continued swimming.

00:00 — Continued swimming.

01:00 — Continued swimming. Bathroom break. Got coffee. Subject still swimming when I returned. TBF 3.6 Hz.

03:00 — Continued swimming. Tried to read a paper. Could not concentrate. Kept looking at the tank.

05:00 — Continued swimming.

07:04 — Trial ended (PI arrival). Duration: 10h 2m. No behavioral state transition observed. Subject transferred to recovery tank.

Notes:

The data are clean. The result is definitive. The controls transitioned. The experimental subjects did not. The mechanism is clear: without astrocytic calcium accumulation, there is no threshold, and without a threshold, there is no state transition.

I should be pleased. This is the paper.

I am not pleased.

I have been watching this fish for ten hours and it has not occurred to me until now to ask whether "inescapable" is a description of the experimental paradigm or of the animal's experience. The protocol calls it inescapable because the virtual walls cannot be overcome. But for the wildtype fish, it isn't — the wildtype fish escapes into quiescence. It surrenders. Surrender is a form of escape.

Subject 14-7 cannot surrender. For this fish, and only this fish, the paradigm is actually inescapable.

Note for revision: rewrite methods section. Change "inescapable-flow paradigm" to something more precise. I don't know what yet.

Addendum (personal, do not include in manuscript):

Called Mom at lunch. Grandmother walked 14,200 steps yesterday. The nurses counted. They count because the number goes in the chart, and the chart goes to the neurologist, and the neurologist uses it to track progression. No one asks whether the steps are an escape or a sentence.

I think they're both. I think the lab notebook is too.
