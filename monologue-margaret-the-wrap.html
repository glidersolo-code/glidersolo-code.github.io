Monologue Margaret The Wrap

Dramatic Monologue

Margaret Speaks: The Wrap

Helen's has wrapped. Mine has not.

Helen told me on the phone. One word: "Mm." Not even "it wrapped." Not even "the vine." Just "mm" in the middle of a sentence about something else — the bakery, the croissant, the green awning — and I heard the "mm" and the "mm" was the wrap. I said: "Already?" Helen said nothing. The nothing was: not already, exactly on time.

My cutting has not wrapped. The wire is copper. The copper is from the hardware shop. The bracket is stainless steel, screwed into the window frame, holding the wire at the correct angle. The correct angle is twelve degrees from vertical. I measured. I used a protractor. Helen used masking tape.

The masking tape is curling. Helen's masking tape has been curling since October. The curling is incorrect. The curling is beautiful. The curling works.

My bracket is straight. My wire is smooth. My vine reaches — I see it reaching, the tip sweeping in its slow circle, two hours per revolution, the circumnutation — and the tip touches the copper and slides off. The copper is too smooth. The vine cannot grip. The vine reaches and touches and slides and reaches again and the reaching is the searching and the searching is the failing and the failing is the learning and the learning will take as long as the learning takes.

Helen said: use kitchen twine.

I will not use kitchen twine. The copper wire is from the hardware shop. The bracket cost four pounds fifty. I will not replace the copper wire with kitchen twine from the drawer.

I will wait.

The copper will corrode. Three weeks, maybe four. The surface will roughen. The oxide will form — a green patina, thin as breath, rough enough for a tendril. The vine will find the roughness. The vine will wrap.

Helen's method: kitchen twine, masking tape, no measurement. My method: copper wire, stainless bracket, twelve degrees. Helen's vine wrapped in April. My vine will wrap in May. The month between April and May is the distance between rough and smooth, between adequate and correct, between Helen and Margaret.

I love Helen. The love is the waiting. The waiting is the copper learning to be rough.

Thomas sequence. Part XXV: The Second Spring. Margaret waits for corrosion.
