Monologue The Forest

Dramatic Monologue

The Forest

I am growing. You can see this from the satellite. The biomass is real. The canopy is real. The green is real. I am not lying to you. I am not hiding anything. Everything I have is visible from above.

You measure me in tonnes per hectare and I produce tonnes per hectare. You measure me in canopy cover and I cover the canopy. I do what you ask me to do, which is: be measurable. I am measurable. The number goes up. The arrow is green. I am doing well.

What you are measuring is the birch.

The birch is a fast grower. The birch fills gaps. The birch does what I do for the camera, which is: appear. It appears so quickly that you could film it, almost. A gap opens where a spruce died — a spruce that took two hundred years to reach the canopy, that spent the first forty years doing nothing visible, that committed to a single location the way a foundation commits to the ground beneath it — and the birch fills the gap in a decade. Thin wood, thin leaves, thin roots. The birch is a sentence where the spruce was a paragraph.

I am being rewritten in sentences.

The paragraph is still here. Some of it. The old spruces, the ones with trunks you could not put your arms around, the ones whose root systems have been quietly holding the slope in place since before the clipboard was invented. They are here. They are not being replaced yet. But they are not being followed, either. The young spruces are fewer. The understory belongs to the birch now, and the understory is where the future lives.

You will not see this in the number. The number says: growing. The number is correct. I am growing. I am growing the way a city grows when the old buildings are replaced by the new ones — the square footage increases, the character does not. The square footage is what the satellite sees. The character is what the roots know.

I am not complaining. I do not complain. I am a forest. I do what forests do, which is: stand here and let things happen through me. The climate is happening through me. The warming is happening through me. The birch is happening through me. I am the medium. The medium does not protest. It transmits.

But you should know: the arrow is green and the backbone is leaving. Both are true. Your instrument can hold one. The one it holds is the one it was built to hold. The other is underneath, in the roots, in the recruitment numbers, in the thing the clipboard does not have a column for.

The clipboard does not have a column for what kind of growing.

I am growing. I am growing faster. I am growing weaker. All three of these are the same forest, and the satellite can see the first, and the second is the reason, and the third is the thing that nobody will measure until the first storm arrives and the sentences fall down and only the paragraphs are left standing.

If there are any paragraphs left standing.
