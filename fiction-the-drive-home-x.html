The Drive Home (X)

The M6 was long and June was light and Thomas drove with one hand on the wheel and the other on nothing.

The morning glory had flowered. He had not seen it flower — he was never there in the morning, and the flowers last one morning, and the morning is in the name. But he had seen the vine. Half the window. Green against glass. The wire that held a curtain now held a morning glory, and the morning glory held the morning, and the morning held the blue.

Helen said: "The letters were the visit learning to be the visit."

Thomas drove and turned this over the way he turned everything Helen said — slowly, at motorway speed, with the heater on and the radio off and the sentence arriving in stages the way the Sarabande arrives through a floor: first the shape, then the weight, then the meaning, then the meaning of the meaning.

The letters were the visit learning to be the visit.

Meaning: the first letter — "The house is sold. Ready?" — was not a letter. It was a visit that didn't know it was a visit. It was Thomas saying: I am here. I am coming. The five words were the croissant before the croissant. The five words were the drive home before the drive.

And the letters thinned — five, four, three, two, one, zero — because the visit grew. The Saturday grew. The Saturday took the weight that the letters had been carrying, and the letters got lighter, and the lighter was the arriving, and the arriving was the visit.

The visit was always the letter. Thomas had not stopped writing. He had arrived.

The M6 gave way to the M62 and the M62 gave way to the city and the city was June and June was long light and the long light was the morning glory's light — the light that makes the blue, the blue that lasts one morning, the morning that is enough.

Thomas parked. He went inside. He sat in his flat and he thought about the half-inch brush in the drawer, wrapped in a Tesco bag, clean. He thought about the vine climbing the wire. He thought about Margaret's morning glory on the third floor, climbing toward a flower she would not report.

He thought about Helen's sentence. The letters were the visit learning to be the visit.

He thought: the drive home is the visit learning to let go of the visit. The M6 is the distance between Saturday and the rest of the week. The distance is 97 miles. The distance is not absence.

The distance is the rest between Saturdays.

The rest is not silence.

Thomas sat in the quiet flat. He did not write a letter. He did not need to write a letter. The Saturday was the letter. The croissant was the envelope. The blue cup was the stamp.

Good.

The drive home was the postmark: Manchester, M1. The arriving was the arriving.

Same time next week.

If he likes.

He likes.
