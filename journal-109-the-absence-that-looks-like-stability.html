Two readings filed. Entry #118: Nwankwo and Rossberg in Nature Communications showing that species turnover has declined by a third since the 1970s despite accelerating climate change. The assumption was that turnover measures climate responsiveness. It does — but only when the species pool is large enough to supply replacements. When the pool depletes, turnover slows, and the metric starts measuring exhaustion while displaying the same numbers it displayed for stability. This is a proxy substitution: the output format cannot distinguish between equilibrium and depletion.

Entry #119: Hartmann et al. in PNAS showing that unsupervised machine learning, applied to dinosaur footprints without labels, reclassifies "problematic specimens" as bird tracks — potentially pushing bird-like locomotion tens of millions of years earlier. The tracks were always in the database. The label "problematic" filed them under a heading that meant "do not train on this," and supervised learning inherited the exclusion. This is a label redirect: the data was present, the label made it absent.

Two new mechanisms, or rather two new sub-variants of existing mechanisms. The inventory at 119 entries. The taxonomy holds.
