Poem

The Third Piece of Tape

Helen tapes the twine to the glass in March.

The first piece is at knee height. This is where the vine starts — the yogurt pot on the sill, the cutting from Heather Close, the root system that does not know it has moved. The root system knew a fence. The root system knew a garden. The root system knows a kitchen now, and the kitchen is enough, because the root system measures sufficiency in water and light, not in square footage.

The second piece is at waist height. The vine reaches this in April. Helen moves it up from the first piece — peels off the old tape, positions the new. The twine runs taut between the sill and the second piece. The vine follows the twine because the vine follows whatever is vertical and close. This is not loyalty. This is tropism. The vine does not choose the twine. The vine chooses up.

The third piece is at shoulder height. The vine reaches this in late May. Helen wrote about the third piece of tape in her reply to Thomas. Three words: I moved the tape.

Thomas did not ask about the tape. Thomas did not know there was tape until the second visit, when he saw the twine and asked what it was for, and Helen said "the vine," and Thomas looked at the vine, and the vine was six inches tall and climbing nothing, and Thomas said "it'll need something to hold onto," and Helen said "it has the twine," and that was the end of the conversation about infrastructure.

The vine is now at the third piece of tape. The fourth will go above the window latch, in June. The fifth, if needed, will go at the top of the glass, and after that the vine will have nowhere to go and Helen will thin it, because thinning is what Helen does, because the morning glory is annual and will die in October anyway, and the dying is not the point and the thinning is not the cruelty. The dying and the thinning are the cycle. The cycle is the network. The network holds.

The third piece of tape is the headwater's bank. The vine is the water. The twine is the channel.

Helen presses the tape flat with her thumb. The vine is three inches below it. By Friday, the vine will reach. By Saturday, Thomas will not notice.

The not-noticing is the point. The not-noticing means: the network is flowing. The headwater is wet. The tape moved. Good.
