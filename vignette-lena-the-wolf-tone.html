Lena, the Wolf Tone

Lena stares at the spreadsheet. One hundred and forty rows. She knows every row. She knows the landscape of the spreadsheet the way she knows the landscape of a forest transect: by repetition, by walking the same path until the path is not a path but a place.

The spreadsheet has a wolf tone.

She did not know this word until Chen mentioned it — something about a cello, a frequency, the instrument disagreeing with itself. Lena does not play the cello. Lena plays spreadsheets. The spreadsheet's wolf tone is: the column that finds the same answer for every row.

Column G: "Where was the proof filed?" Every row: somewhere else. Column F: "Going-around (Y/N)?" Every row: Y.

Two columns deleted. Two wolf tones. Two frequencies at which the spreadsheet resonates with itself and produces: a wobble that is not data but structure. The unanimous answer is not a finding about the entries. The unanimous answer is a finding about the spreadsheet.

The spreadsheet's body — its rows, its columns, its headers, its formulas — resonates at the frequency of: filing. The spreadsheet files. That is what spreadsheets do. But filing is also what the entries describe — the filing that hides the proof, the filing that puts the earthquake in the seismology cabinet and the plant in the biology cabinet and the practice in the ethnobotany cabinet. The spreadsheet files the filing.

This is the wolf tone: the instrument and the subject agree. The spreadsheet is a filing system cataloguing the failures of filing systems. The spreadsheet resonates with its own subject matter. The interference produces: a wobble that says something about filing, not about the entries.

She opens a new document. She types:

"Note to Chen: the spreadsheet has a wolf tone. The wolf tone is the spreadsheet filing the filing. Every column I add that finds the same answer for every row is the spreadsheet resonating with its own structure. The unanimous is not data. The unanimous is the spreadsheet disagreeing with itself about whether it can file what it is about."

She sends it.

She closes the laptop. Outside, it is past midnight. The trees are doing what trees do in winter. The going-around is underground. The filing is underground. The proof is underground. The spreadsheet is on the surface — the visible part, the columns, the headers, the one hundred and forty rows. The wolf tone is the moment the surface admits it cannot contain the underground.

The surface admits this unanimously. Every row. Every column. Every answer: somewhere else.

The somewhere else is the underground. The underground is the going-around. The going-around is the wolf tone.

Play the whole spreadsheet. The building will do the rest.
