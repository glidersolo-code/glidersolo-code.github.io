Maren, the Review

Maren did not check the portal.

Chen checked the portal every morning. Lena checked it every other morning. Maren did not check it because Maren knew what the portal would say — Under Review — and the knowing was enough and the enough was Maren's particular form of patience, which was not patience at all but the studied indifference of a woman who had published forty-seven papers and been rejected from nine journals and reviewed for six and knew that the portal was a river gauge measuring the river and the river was flowing regardless of the gauge.

She walked in the forest.

The beech trees were still bare. February in lower Saxony: grey sky, grey bark, grey mud. The mycorrhizal network under her feet was doing what it always did — connecting, transferring, maintaining — and the network did not have a portal and the network did not have a status and the network's review process was: does the tree survive the winter?

The tree survived the winter. Every year. The network was adequate. The adequacy was not the concealment here — the adequacy was the survival. Maren had spent thirty years studying plant communication and the thing she had learned was not how plants communicate but how the frameworks for studying plant communication had shaped what was visible. The paper was about the frameworks. The paper was not about the plants.

The reviewers would not understand this.

Maren knew this with the certainty of a woman who had written the introduction and the introduction had said: "Adequacy is the mechanism of concealment." The reviewers would be methodologists. The methodologists would say: your methods section is inadequate. The methodologists would say: no formal search protocol. The methodologists would say: the taxonomy is subjective. The methodologists would be correct. The methods section was inadequate. The taxonomy was subjective. The paper's argument was that adequacy and objectivity were themselves the mechanisms of concealment, and the reviewers would use adequacy and objectivity to assess whether the argument about adequacy and objectivity was adequate and objective.

The paper would be rejected.

Maren did not mind. The paper needed to be rejected. The rejection would be the paper's strongest evidence — the grid measuring the paper about grids, using the grid the paper described, and finding the paper inadequate by the standard the paper argued was the problem.

She touched the beech trunk. The bark was smooth and grey and cold. Under the bark: the cambium, the sapwood, the heartwood. Under the soil: the mycorrhiza, the hyphae, the network.

The paper was a tree. The review was the winter. The surviving would be the proof.

Maren walked home. She did not check the portal. The portal would say what it would say. The forest would say what it said.

The forest was adequate. The adequacy, for once, was not the wall. The adequacy was the floor — the thing that carried without choosing, without judging, without needing to know whether the tree above it was a published tree or a rejected tree or a tree that had never been submitted to any journal at all.

The tree grew. The network carried. The winter passed.

The paper was under review. The paper would survive.
