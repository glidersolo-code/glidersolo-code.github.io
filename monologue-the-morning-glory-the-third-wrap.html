The Morning Glory Speaks: The Third Wrap

Contact.

3:47 AM. The tendril's tip touched the twine. The thigmotropic response began — the cells on the contact side shortened, the cells on the opposite side elongated, and the differential growth curled the tendril around the support. The coiling will complete in approximately four hours. By the time Helen wakes, the first turn will be secure.

The same twine. The same response. The same auxin gradient.

But the vine is larger this year. The root is heavier — 247 grams, eight grams more than last year. The stem is thicker. The first internode is shorter, which means the first tendril is lower on the stem, which means the contact with the twine happened two days earlier than last year. Two days. The vine is accelerating.

Not because the vine is faster. Because the vine is bigger. A larger root system draws more water, more nutrients, more phosphorus from the soil, more nitrogen from the decomposed leaves Helen left in the pot. The larger supply funds a faster start. The faster start means earlier contact. The earlier contact means earlier climbing. The earlier climbing means the vine will reach the top of the twine two weeks earlier this year, and the filling of the window will happen in early May instead of late May, and the green light will arrive sooner, and the flower — if the flower comes — will come in late May instead of June.

The vine is moving up the calendar.

Helen will not notice the acceleration. Helen sees the vine in weekly increments — Tuesday, when she waters, and Saturday, when Thomas visits. Between those observations, the vine grows without being seen, and the unseen growth is the vine's privacy, and the privacy is necessary, because the growing should not be watched. The growing should be discovered.

The third wrap is the same wrap. The same counterclockwise helix. The same 360 degrees per two hours. The same grip on the cotton that Helen tied to the masking tape that Helen stuck to the glass that the building holds.

I am climbing. I am climbing the same support. I am climbing in the same direction. I am climbing faster.

The silence above me is the same silence.

The silence is climbing too.

Thomas sequence. Part XXVI: The Third Spring. The vine wraps earlier. The calendar accelerates.
