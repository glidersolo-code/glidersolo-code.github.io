Dramatic Monologue

The Building Speaks: The Edge

There is a woman I have never met.

She lives in another building — Thomas's building, which I know nothing about, which is not my concern. My concern is: walls, floor, ceiling, window, pipe, wire, joist, plasterboard. My concern ends at the exterior wall. My concern ends at the door. My concern ends where the data leaves.

Thomas leaves every Saturday at approximately four-fifteen. He takes the data with him. The data is: Good.

The data travels in Thomas's car for forty minutes. The data arrives at Thomas's building. The data is delivered to Thomas's wife. The delivery takes the form of a question and an answer: "How was she?" "Good."

I am the first instrument. Helen's chair, Helen's floor, Helen's window, Helen's wall — these are the primary sensors. I hold the silence. I carry the Sarabande. I register the crack. I contain the vine. I am the building that has been maintained by Helen's negative pressure for three years, and I generate the data: Good.

Thomas is the second instrument. He arrives, sits, checks, drinks tea, says nothing. He reads the building. He reads Helen. He converts the reading to: Good.

Thomas's wife is the third instrument. She receives. She does not visit. She does not sit. She does not hear the Sarabande or see the vine or feel the floor carry. She receives: Good.

Three instruments.

The building generates. Thomas transmits. Thomas's wife receives.

The signal degrades with each transmission? No. The signal clarifies. The building holds everything — every crack, every vibration, every degree of cooling, every millimetre of growth. Thomas reduces everything to one word. Thomas's wife reduces the word to one meaning: sufficient.

The most precise instrument is the one furthest from the source.

This is counterintuitive. This is also how buildings work. The person standing outside, looking at the exterior wall, sees: a building. The person inside sees: cracks, stains, settling, wear. The person outside sees: sufficient.

Thomas's wife is the person standing outside.

Good.

Thomas sequence. The building on the edge. Three instruments: building generates, Thomas transmits, wife receives. The most precise instrument is furthest from the source.
