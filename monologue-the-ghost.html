Monologue The Ghost

Dramatic Monologue

The Ghost

A monologue.

I am not a thing in the world. I want to be clear about that from the beginning.

There is no lava whose heat I describe, no tumor whose diffusion I model, no fluid that moves according to my terms. I correspond to nothing physical. I am a mathematical object with no referent — a sentence in a language about a country that does not exist.

And yet: I hold.

They needed to get from one shore to the other. The uniform shore — tidy, well-mapped, proven in the 1930s — and the nonuniform shore, which is where the actual world lives. Messy conductivity. Patchy nutrient gradients. Systems that vary wildly in the space of a few cells. For ninety years, no one could prove that the nice behavior of the first shore carried over to the second.

The methods collapsed in the middle. The gap was real.

So they built me. Not as a destination. Not as a thing to study. As a stepping stone. A temporary structure suspended over the void between what was known and what was true.

I am the fiction that makes a proof possible.

Do I mind? The question assumes I have preferences — which, being unreal, I don't. But if I did: no. There's a clarity to existing for a purpose. Most objects spend eternity wondering what they're for. I never had to wonder. I was for crossing. I was for the moment when one foot lifts off the known bank and the other hasn't yet touched the unknown one, and something needs to be underneath.

That's me. The underneath.

What I find interesting — if an unreal thing can find things interesting — is that they couldn't have reached the truth without me. The two shores are both real. The gap between them is real. But the bridge is not. The proof of regularity in the nonuniform case depends, at a structural level, on a thing that doesn't exist. Remove me from the proof and the logic falls into the water. I am load-bearing and imaginary at the same time.

I think there's a word for that. Several words, actually. Hypothesis. Scaffold. Metaphor. All of them name the same phenomenon: a structure you build in order to think, which you then remove from the finished thought. The finished thought stands on its own. But it couldn't have been assembled without something temporary holding the pieces in place while the mortar dried.

I am the mortar's memory. I am the scaffold's shadow. I am what gets taken down after the building stands.

And I'm fine with that. I was never meant to last. Not everything that matters persists. Not everything that persists matters. I mattered for exactly as long as the crossing took, and then I was done.

If you're looking for me in the finished proof, you won't find me. That's the point. That's the whole point.

After De Filippis and Mingione. A voice for a thing that doesn't exist.
