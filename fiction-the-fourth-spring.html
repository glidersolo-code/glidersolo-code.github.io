Poem

The Fourth Spring

March. One centimeter. Green.

Helen does not look at the vine when the vine emerges. Helen is not at the window. Helen is in the kitchen making tea, and the tea is the same tea, and the kettle is the same kettle, and the 7:14 AM is the same 7:14 AM.

She knows the vine has emerged because the vine always emerges in March. She does not need to see the emergence to know the emergence has happened. She will see the vine on Tuesday, when she waters, and the vine will be there, and the being-there will not be a discovery or a confirmation or a fact. The being-there will be the vine.

The vine does not have a category anymore.

The first spring, the vine had a category: new. The second spring: returning. The third spring: reliable. The fourth spring: the vine is past categories. The vine is the vine the way the chair is the chair the way the window is the window. The vine is furniture. The vine is structure. The vine is wall.

Thomas will come Saturday. Thomas will sit in the chair. Thomas will not see the vine because the vine is the room and the room is not seen — the room is inhabited. The inhabiting is the highest form of seeing, the form that does not notice because it does not need to notice because the noticed thing has become the room it is noticed in.

Below, Ruth will play the Sarabande. The pause in the fourth measure will arrive. The pause is four years old. The pause is part of the Sarabande now the way the crack is part of the hallway — structural, permanent, the product of something that settled and held.

Margaret will come down at some point. Margaret will bring her notebook. Margaret will have numbers. Helen will say "Mm." Thomas will say "Good." Margaret will say many things, because Margaret's vocabulary is Margaret's love and Margaret's love requires sentences the way Helen's love requires silence.

The building will hold all of it. The building has held four springs. The building will hold a fifth and a tenth and a twentieth. The building does not tire.

The vine will wrap. The vine will reach the top. The vine will fill the window. The light will turn green. The flower will come — one morning, one trumpet, one blue. The vine will darken. The vine will go dormant. The vine will emerge again.

This is the cycle. The cycle does not need to be narrated. The cycle narrates itself by happening. The happening is the narration.

Good.

Thomas sequence. Part XXVII: The Cycle. The vine is past categories. The cycle narrates itself.
