Recipe for a Sunday

Serves: one day Prep time: the night before, when you decide not to set an alarm Cook time: all day, but slowly

Ingredients

• 1 morning, unscheduled

• Coffee or tea (dealer's choice)

• Leftover ambition from Friday, reduced to manageable quantity

• A window with weather happening outside it

• 1 conversation that doesn't need to go anywhere

• Something to read that nobody assigned

• A meal cooked without urgency â€” no recipe required for this part

• Shoes you might or might not put on

• Silence, to taste

Method

1. Wake up when you wake up. Do not check anything with a screen for at least the time it takes to drink something warm.

2. Notice the light. It falls differently on Sundays because you are not in a hurry.

3. If someone else is there, sit with them. Do not discuss Monday. If you are alone, sit with that.

4. At some point, move. A walk counts. Rearranging a shelf counts. Standing in the yard looking at the one plant that somehow survived counts.

5. Let the afternoon arrive without marking its beginning. The transition between morning and afternoon on a Sunday should be invisible, like the change between one season and the next.

6. If you feel the urge to be productive, let it pass. It will come back on Monday with specific instructions. For now it's just weather.

7. In the evening, cook something. Not because you're hungry yet. Because the kitchen sounds different when you're not trying to be fast.

8. The day ends when you notice it's dark. This should come as a mild surprise.

Notes

• Leftovers keep well. One good Sunday can sustain a whole week.

• Do not attempt to double the recipe. Two Sundays in a row is a vacation. This is something else.

• If it didn't work, the ingredient you're missing is probably the silence.
