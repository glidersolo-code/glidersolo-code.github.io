Thomas's Letter

Mum,

I went past Heather Close today. I know you said not to. I know you said the house is theirs now and looking at someone else's house is not the same as looking at yours and the difference matters. You're right. The difference matters.

Margaret came with me. I didn't ask her to. She was at the shops when I parked and she saw the car and she said "you're going to the house" and I said "just driving past" and she got in and we both knew I wasn't just driving past.

The clematis is dormant. It will flower in July. I know this because Dad planted it the year I started school, and I know when things flower the same way I know when things were planted, which is to say: because you told me, and the things you tell me stay.

The fence needs painting.

Margaret talked about Dad. I don't think I've heard her talk about him before. She talked about the fence the way you talk about the morning glory — not as a thing but as a decision someone made. She said the fence was a love letter. She said nobody builds a fence for themselves. She said the paint and the May and the tea at eleven were a language, and the language was maintenance, and maintenance was the love.

I didn't know Margaret thought about him.

I think maybe I didn't know Margaret thought about anything except square footage and almond croissants. That's not fair. I know that's not fair. Margaret thinks about things the way the clematis grows — slowly, and without announcing it, and three flowers in July.

The morning glory will need planting again in March. I know this because you told me. I know you chose an annual on purpose. I know the purpose is not what Margaret thinks the purpose is. Margaret thinks you chose something that dies. You chose something that arrives.

The kettle whistle starts at E-flat. Dad verified this. I don't know why I'm telling you something you already know. Maybe because the things we already know are the ones worth saying out loud, in the same way the fence is worth painting even though it was painted last May and will need painting next May. The repetition is not redundancy. The repetition is the grammar.

Come Saturday if you want. I'll bring nothing.

Thomas

Epistolary #9. Thomas sequence, piece #38. Thomas writes to Helen after visiting the fence with Margaret.
