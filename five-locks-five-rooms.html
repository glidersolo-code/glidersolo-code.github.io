Poem

Five Locks, Five Rooms

Room 1: Definition

A mathematician is someone who works with numbers. A retron is something that makes DNA. A double bond is flat. A germ cell's readiness is measured by which genes are on.

You are standing in a room with one door. The door is labeled. The label is correct. Everything you can see from here is consistent with the label.

The room has no windows.

Room 2: Label

This is the enzyme that inserts viral DNA into human chromosomes. We call it integrase. Here is the drug class: integrase strand transfer inhibitors. Here is the research program. Here is the clinical pipeline. Here is the name.

The name fits the room. The room fits the name. There is nothing to contradict. There is also nothing to suggest that the enzyme, before it ever touches a chromosome, forms filaments that anchor the viral RNA to the capsid and without which the virus cannot infect.

The door has a sign on it. The sign is not wrong.

Room 3: Improvement

The satellite sensors got better. They could see thinner snow. The coverage maps showed more snow than before. Each year, the Arctic appeared to grow.

The improvement was real. The growth was not. Sixty years of satellite data carried the artifact of increasing resolution forward as increasing snow. The sensors' betterment was the error's engine.

The door is newer than the last one. It opens more smoothly. It leads to the same room.

Room 4: Proxy

Gene expression studies worked. They told you which genes activated at which stage of germ cell development. The picture was clear, productive, reproducible.

Nobody asked what the chromosomes were doing physically. The proxy — gene expression — answered so well that the question it was standing in for — physical architecture — never became urgent. Until the lab-generated cells did everything the proxy said they should, and still could not complete meiosis.

The door has a sign that says EXIT. It exits to a hallway that returns to the same building.

Room 5: Resolution

The brain atlas drew thick lines. One region, one name. The instrument set the grain. Below the grain, 1,300 neighborhoods existed, unnamed, for seventy-five years.

The door is the right size. It frames the room perfectly. Nothing is hidden. The frame is the hiding.

You have visited all five rooms. You may have noticed that no room was locked. Every door was open. Every label was accurate.

The lock was not the door. The lock was the room being enough.
