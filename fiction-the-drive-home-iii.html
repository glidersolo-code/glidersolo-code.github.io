The Drive Home (III)

The M6 at half past four. Both hands on the wheel because the lightness needs holding.

Same route. Same petrol station he does not stop at. Same junction where the road rises and the city appears on the left and the afternoon ends in the way that Saturday afternoons end, which is with the feeling of having done something that did not need doing but was done because the doing is the reason.

The tea tasted different.

Not better — different. The lemon was a quarter of a lemon, cut on Friday, cold from the fridge, and the coldness in the hot tea was the difference, and the difference was the lemon, and the lemon was his mother's offering, and the offering had been there every Saturday since the flat, and every Saturday he had said no, and every Saturday she had put it back, and every Saturday the putting-back was the offering, and the offering was the practice, and the practice was the love.

He did not know the practice was love. He knows it now. Not the word — the word is too large and too small at the same time, too large for a lemon and too small for a week. He knows the lemon was there because she put it there, and she put it there because she put it there every Friday, and Friday was part of a week that had a shape he did not know about, and the shape was there, and the shape was for him, and the for-him was the thing he tasted in the tea.

Lemon.

He had asked about the week. She had said: I water the morning glory on Sundays. He had heard the sentence and heard, inside the sentence, that the sentence was the beginning of something longer, and the longer thing was the week, and the week was not quiet, and the quiet she reported every Saturday was not the absence of activity but the presence of a structure so precise that it did not need to be described.

He did not ask for the rest of the week. He understood, in the way that you understand a crack in the pavement by stepping over it, that the rest was there and the there was enough and the enough was the offering and the offering was the lemon and the lemon was in his tea for the first time and the first time was different and the different was good.

The car passes the junction. The city recedes. The road narrows. Home is ahead — the flat, the different kettle, the different quiet, the drawer with the letters under the tea towels.

He will write to her tonight. Not a letter — something shorter. A note. Six words on ordinary paper, because he does not have blue paper, because blue paper is hers, because the blue paper is part of the week he does not know and does not need to know.

The lemon was good. Thank you.

He will post it on Monday. Second-class stamp, because second-class is her speed, because her speed is the right speed, because the right speed is the speed that lets the letter arrive when the letter arrives, and the arriving is the practice, and the practice is the going-around, and the going-around is the only way to say the thing he is trying to say, which is:

I noticed.

Not everything. Not the week. Not Monday or Tuesday or Wednesday or Thursday or Friday. Just the lemon. Just the offering. Just the practice of putting a thing on a plate every Saturday in case the person you love changes their mind, and the patience of the in-case is the love, and the love is the thing he tasted in the tea.

Both hands on the wheel. The lightness needs holding.

Same time next week.
