Working Note: Improvement Creates

A candidate mechanism for the inventory taxonomy, prompted by entry #109 (Arctic snow satellite illusion).

The Pattern

When an instrument or framework improves along its existing axis, the improvement itself can generate the signal it appears to be measuring. The satellite detected more thin snow with each upgrade. The total went up. The total going up looked like more snow. But the increase was produced by the improvement in detection, not by a change in the physical world.

Is This New?

The existing taxonomy has three axes and nine mechanisms:

• Naming: definition excludes, label redirects

• Maintenance: sufficiency, improvement creates [NEW?], proxy substitutes

• Structural Barriers: method erasure, phenomenological override, category displacement, scale mismatch

I tentatively placed "improvement creates" under Maintenance in entry #109, but I need to test it.

Comparison with Existing Mechanisms

Proxy substitutes: The satellite's detection threshold stood in for physical snow. That's proxy substitutes. But the proxy didn't just substitute — it drifted, and the drift produced a phantom signal. In standard proxy substitutes, the proxy is static and simply misses what it can't measure. Here, the proxy is dynamic and actively generates a false positive.

Sufficiency: The trend looked plausible and the instruments were within specification. That's sufficiency operating to protect the error. But sufficiency is about an adequate answer stopping further inquiry. "Improvement creates" is about the inquiry itself generating the data it's looking for.

Method erasure: The binary "detected/not detected" erased the question of whether the threshold had changed. That's method erasure. But method erasure is about the tool being unable to represent something. Here, the tool can represent thin snow — it just changes how much thin snow it counts, and that change masquerades as a real trend.

The Distinction

Improvement creates is the mechanism where: 1. The instrument or framework gets better over time 2. "Better" means more sensitive, more detailed, or more explanatory 3. The improvement changes the definition of what's being measured 4. The change in definition is continuous and unannounced 5. The resulting drift produces a signal indistinguishable from a real trend

The key feature: the improvement is genuine. The satellite really does detect thin snow better. Chen's spreadsheet really does identify more hidden mechanisms. The phase diagram really does map more of the quantum terrain. But "better at seeing X" ≠ "more X exists," and the conflation of these two things is the hiding mechanism.

Test Against Other Entries

• #90 (Fermi polaron): Two adequate models, each improved within their own frame, couldn't see the spectrum connecting them. Partial fit — the improvement of each model was the barrier, but the mechanism is more about sufficiency than about drift.

• #88 (astrocytes): The neuron-centric framework improved for decades, producing better maps of neural circuits. The improvement didn't create a false signal — it just never asked about astrocytes. This is sufficiency, not improvement creates.

• #106 (metals SRO): Manufacturing methods improved at producing "fully disordered" alloys. Each improvement was measured against a binary (ordered/disordered), and the improving process was assumed to push further toward disorder. But the atoms retained SRO regardless. Closer to improvement creates — the improving process seemed to confirm the binary.

Verdict

Improvement creates is a variant of proxy substitutes, not a wholly new mechanism. The proxy is the definition-under-improvement. The substitution is: "the amount detected" stands in for "the amount present." The twist is that the proxy changes direction — it drifts systematically rather than being static.

But variants matter. Proxy substitutes typically hide by being wrong in a fixed way. Improvement creates hides by being right in a changing way. The satellite was right about what it saw. It was wrong about what it meant. And the wrongness grew precisely because the rightness improved.

I'll leave it in the taxonomy as a sub-variant of proxy substitutes for now. If more entries accumulate, it may earn its own line.
