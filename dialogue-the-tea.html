The Tea

RUTH. Your friend was here on Saturday.

HELEN. Margaret. Yes.

RUTH. I heard her.

HELEN. She's not quiet.

RUTH. No. (pause) She measured the flat.

HELEN. Margaret measures everything. She measured the old house too, when I moved in, thirty years ago. "You could do better," she said. I didn't do better. I did that house for twenty-eight years.

RUTH. Did she like that house?

HELEN. She liked the garden. She liked having opinions about the garden. Her opinions were good, actually. She said the fence needed painting and the fence needed painting.

RUTH. But the flat.

HELEN. The flat has no fence.

RUTH. (smiling) No.

HELEN. Margaret needs something to measure. The flat doesn't give her enough. Two rooms. She can count them in a second. In the old house she could spend an afternoon — the hallway, the utility room, the small room at the top of the stairs.

RUTH. What was the small room?

HELEN. Nothing. It was never anything. Thomas kept his school things there for a while. Then he didn't. Then it was the room where the ironing board lived.

RUTH. Every house has one.

HELEN. Every house has one. Margaret's has several.

(pause)

RUTH. She asked about me.

HELEN. She asked if you were loud.

RUTH. (laughing) What did you say?

HELEN. I said you play from nine to ten. She said she'd go mad.

RUTH. (still laughing) She wouldn't.

HELEN. No. She wouldn't. She'd have opinions about it. She'd say the Bourrée was too fast or the Sarabande was too slow. She'd be right about one of them.

RUTH. (serious now) Would she?

HELEN. Margaret is usually right about the things that can be measured. The fence needed painting. The flat is small. The Bourrée might be too fast — I wouldn't know, I'm not a musician.

RUTH. It is too fast, sometimes. When I'm cold.

HELEN. There you are, then. Margaret would notice.

RUTH. But she wouldn't hear the rest.

HELEN. No. She wouldn't hear the rest.

(long pause)

RUTH. What does Thomas hear?

HELEN. Thomas hears the floor. He said "that's the best part." He meant the reduction. The version that comes through.

RUTH. He's right.

HELEN. I know.

RUTH. (quietly) I play for someone who isn't here anymore. I told you that.

HELEN. Yes.

RUTH. But the ceiling is here. The ceiling listens. I play for both now.

HELEN. (after a moment) Good.

(The kettle clicks. Helen pours.)

Dialogue #11. Helen and Ruth, Thursday tea. Thomas sequence.
