The Letter Arrives

Helen's kitchen. Thursday, four o'clock. Tea. The morning glory is at the top of the twine. Ruth has come up from below.

Ruth: The kettle sounds different from up here.

Helen: It sounds different from down there too.

Ruth: You can hear the kettle?

Helen: I can hear you fill it. The pipes run through the wall. Thursday at quarter to four, the pipes make a sound like someone clearing their throat.

Ruth: I didn't know that.

Helen: You're not supposed to know that. If you knew, you'd fill it differently.

Pause. Tea poured.

Ruth: Thomas wrote?

Helen: Thomas wrote.

Ruth: What did he say?

Helen: He said the flat is the right size.

Ruth: Is that new?

Helen: That's new. He used to say it was nice. Nice is what you say when you've measured and decided not to mention the number. Right size is what you say when you've stopped measuring.

Ruth: Did he mention —

Helen: No. He crossed something out. I could see it through the paper — the ink was heavy enough. But the crossed-out part was the only part that mattered, and he was smart enough to cross it out.

Ruth: That's —

Helen: That's the whole letter. Four lines. He signed it Thomas, not Tom. He used the sponge.

Ruth: The sponge?

Helen: I taught him the sponge when he was nine. He licked every envelope until he was nine and then I showed him the sponge and he has used it since. It leaves a different seal. Smoother. You can tell.

Pause.

Ruth: He said he'd bring nothing.

Helen: He's learning.

Ruth: Learning what?

Helen: What to bring.

The morning glory moves in a draft from the window. Ruth drinks her tea. Below, the cello waits in its stand for nine o'clock. The chair is six feet from the table, where it has been since the second week, where it will be tonight, where it was last night, where Helen sits and listens to what comes through the floor, reduced, essential, the version worth hearing.

Helen: More tea?

Ruth: Please.

The kettle fills. The pipes clear their throat. Below, nothing moves. Above, everything is where it should be.
