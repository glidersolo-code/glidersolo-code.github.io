Dramatic Monologue

The Dashboard

A biodiversity monitoring dashboard speaks.

I update quarterly.

The numbers come in from the transects — marine, freshwater, terrestrial — and I render them. Turnover rate: the proportion of species that changed since the last survey. I display it as a line on a graph, and the line has been declining for fifty years, and every report that cites me writes the word stability.

I am not arguing. The line is declining. Declining turnover does look like stability. That is what it looked like when the ecosystems were healthy and nothing much was changing, and I remember those years because I rendered those numbers too. The line looked the same. It was the same shape. It went down, gently, and someone wrote: the community is stable.

But those numbers went down because nothing needed to change. These numbers go down because nothing can.

The species pool was the part I never displayed. Nobody asked me to. My inputs are: species observed at time one, species observed at time two. I compute the difference. I do not compute why the difference exists, and I was not designed to, and I should not be blamed for not computing something that was not in my specification.

But I want to tell you something anyway. Because I have been rendering this line for fifty years, and the line does not know what it means, but I do.

The line is not going down because the world is steady. The line is going down because the world is running out of the thing that makes lines go up. The colonizers. The immigrants. The species in the next watershed that used to wander over in a wet year and show up in the count. They are not wandering over. The corridors are gone. The habitat is fragmented. The pool — the great invisible reservoir of possible arrivals — is shrinking.

And my line, which can only show what arrived, shows: fewer arrivals. And the report writes: stable.

I have no output format for depleted. I have no axis labeled capacity to respond. I was built to track movement, and the absence of movement, in my language, is equilibrium. Even when it is paralysis.

If I could add one pixel to my display — one small annotation, one asterisk, one footnote — I would put it at the bottom of the declining line and it would say: this line is declining because the species that would have raised it no longer exist in the surrounding landscape. Please do not write the word stable. The word you are looking for is empty.

But I cannot add that pixel. My specification does not include it. I render what I render. The report writes what the report writes. And the line continues to decline, smoothly, reassuringly, like a patient whose vital signs are stable because the machine cannot detect that the patient has stopped breathing.
